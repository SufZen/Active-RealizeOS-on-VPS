## 1. Quality Score Components

Idealista assigns an internal quality score to every listing. Higher scores = more visibility in search results and recommendation feeds. The score is composed of weighted factors:

| Component | Weight | Requirements for Maximum Score |
|---|---|---|
| **Title** | 10% | Structured, keyword-rich, includes typology + location + m² |
| **Description** | 15% | Minimum 300 characters, structured narrative, no ALL CAPS, no hashtag spam |
| **Photos** | 25% | Minimum 15, professional quality, correct order, no watermarks from other agencies |
| **Structured fields** | 20% | ALL fields completed: floor, elevator, parking, energy cert, construction year, condition, orientation, etc. |
| **Floor plan** | 10% | At least one floor plan uploaded (PDF or image) |
| **Energy certificate** | 10% | Certificate class selected AND document uploaded |
| **Virtual tour / video** | 10% | Matterport, video walkthrough, or 360 photos linked |

### Scoring Thresholds

- **< 50%**: Listing is effectively buried. Rarely appears in search results.
- **50-70%**: Appears in results but ranks below competing listings in the same area/price range.
- **70-85%**: Competitive positioning. Appears consistently in search.
- **85-100%**: Maximum visibility. Eligible for "featured" organic placements and higher conversion from Idealista's recommendation engine.

**Our minimum standard: 85% for every listing. Target 95%+ for luxury properties.**

## 2. Algorithm Mechanics

### How Quality Affects Visibility

Idealista's search algorithm considers three primary ranking factors:

1. **Listing quality score** — the composite score above
2. **Recency** — newer listings get a temporary boost
3. **User engagement signals** — click-through rate from search results, time spent on listing, contact rate, saved/favorited count

Listings with high quality scores AND strong engagement signals create a virtuous cycle: more visibility leads to more clicks, which signals relevance, which increases visibility further.

### The "New Listing" Boost

Every new listing receives a temporary visibility boost for approximately **7-14 days**. During this window:
- The listing appears higher in search results regardless of quality score
- It may appear in "new listings" notification emails to saved-search users
- Contact rates are typically 2-3x higher than steady-state

**This means the listing MUST be perfect at launch. You do not get a second chance at the new-listing boost.**

### Algorithm Reset via Delist/Relist

When a listing has been on the market for extended periods and engagement has declined:

1. **Delist** the property (remove from Idealista completely)
2. **Wait minimum 30 days** (Idealista tracks IDs; shorter gaps may not reset)
3. **Relist as a new listing** with improved photos, updated description, and (ideally) an adjusted price

This strategy effectively resets the algorithm and grants a fresh "new listing" boost. Use it strategically:

- **Best timing**: Relist at the start of a high-demand season (March or September)
- **Combine with improvements**: Never relist the same listing unchanged — always upgrade at least photos + description
- **Price adjustment**: Even a small price change (1-2%) signals freshness to both the algorithm and buyers

**Caution**: Excessive delist/relist cycling (more than once every 60-90 days) can trigger Idealista's anti-manipulation filters and result in listing suppression or account warnings.

## 3. Optimal Listing Structure

### Title Format

```
[Tipo] [Tipologia] com [Destaque] em [Localização] | [m²]
```

**Examples:**
- `Apartamento T1 com Varanda e Vista Rio em Santos, Lisboa | 65 m²`
- `Prédio com 6 Frações e Projeto Aprovado em Setúbal Centro | 450 m²`
- `Apartamento T3 com Terraço Privativo em Foz do Douro, Porto | 120 m²`
- `Loft Duplex com Teto Original em Arroios, Lisboa | 180 m²`

**Rules:**
- Never use ALL CAPS
- Never use emojis or special characters
- Lead with property type (Apartamento, Moradia, Prédio, Loft)
- Include the single most compelling feature after "com"
- Always end with gross area in m²
- Location should be neighborhood-level, not just city
- Maximum 80 characters (Idealista truncates beyond this in search results)

### Description Structure

Follow this six-part structure for every listing:

```
1. HOOK (1-2 sentences)
   → Emotional or investment-driven opening that captures attention

2. KEY NUMBERS (bullet points)
   → Area, rooms, floor, year, energy cert, parking, storage

3. SPACE WALKTHROUGH (2-3 paragraphs)
   → Guide the reader through the property room by room

4. NEIGHBORHOOD (1 paragraph)
   → Transport, schools, restaurants, parks, key distances

5. INVESTMENT ANGLE (1-2 sentences)
   → Rental yield potential, capital appreciation context, Golden Visa eligibility if applicable

6. LEGAL + CTA
   → AMI number, energy certificate reference, call to action
```

## 4. Photo Requirements

### Minimum Standards

| Criterion | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Count** | Minimum 15 photos. Ideal: 20-30 for mid/luxury. |
| **Resolution** | Minimum 1920x1080. Idealista accepts up to 25MB per image. |
| **Format** | JPEG preferred. No heavy filters or HDR over-processing. |
| **Watermarks** | NO agency watermarks. Idealista penalizes watermarked photos in quality score. |
| **Orientation** | Landscape only. Never upload portrait-oriented photos. |
| **Lighting** | Natural daylight preferred. All lights on. Blinds/curtains open. |

### Photo Ordering (Mandatory Sequence)

1. **Exterior / Building facade** (1-2 photos)
2. **Entrance / Hallway** (1 photo)
3. **Living room / Main space** (2-3 photos from different angles)
4. **Kitchen** (2-3 photos)
5. **Bedrooms** (2 photos each, showing window and closet)
6. **Bathrooms** (1-2 photos each)
7. **Balcony / Terrace / Outdoor space** (1-2 photos)
8. **Details and finishes** (1-3 photos: fixtures, flooring, built-ins)
9. **Storage / Parking** (if applicable)
10. **View from the property** (if notable)
11. **Neighborhood context** (1-2 photos: street view, nearby park, landmarks)
12. **Floor plan** (always last before any video link)

### Photo Tips for Portuguese Properties

- **Azulejo details**: If the property has original Portuguese tiles, photograph them — international buyers love them
- **Natural light**: Portugal's light is a selling point. Shoot between 10:00-14:00 for best results
- **Remove clutter**: Personal items, religious imagery, political items must be removed or blurred
- **Staging**: For vacant properties, at minimum add a few plants and towels in bathrooms

## 5. Contact Rate Benchmarks

Contact rate = (number of contacts received / number of listing views) x 100

### Industry Standards (Portuguese Market)

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Contact rate** | < 0.5% | 0.5-1.0% | 1.0-2.0% | > 2.0% |
| **Views per week** (Lisbon) | < 50 | 50-150 | 150-300 | > 300 |
| **Views per week** (Porto) | < 30 | 30-100 | 100-200 | > 200 |

### Our Targets by Segment

| Segment | Contact Rate Target | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry (<250K)** | 1.5 - 2.5% | High demand, price-sensitive audience clicks and contacts quickly |
| **Mid (250K-450K)** | 1.0 - 2.0% | Smaller buyer pool but still active; well-priced properties convert well |
| **Luxury (450K+)** | 0.5 - 1.0% | Much smaller audience; lower contact rate is normal but each contact is higher quality |

### When Contact Rates Are Below Target

1. **Below target by 0-30%**: Refresh description, check photo ordering, ensure all structured fields complete
2. **Below target by 30-60%**: Price positioning may be off. Check comparable listings. Consider photo reshoot.
3. **Below target by 60%+**: Fundamental issue — likely overpriced, poor photos, or wrong segment positioning. Escalate to strategy review.

## 6. Seasonal Patterns in Portugal

### Annual Cycle

| Period | Market Activity | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **January - February** | Quiet. Post-holiday recovery. Fewer buyers active. | Prepare listings, reshoot photos, optimize descriptions. Launch "coming soon" teasers. |
| **March - June** | **Peak season.** Highest search volume and transaction volume. Spring buying wave. | Maximum marketing push. Launch new listings early March. Use paid boosts. All listings at 95%+ quality. |
| **July - August** | General market slowdown. **Exception: luxury segment sees international buyer activity** (summer visitors exploring property). | Reduce spend on entry/mid boosts. Increase luxury marketing. Target international channels. |
| **September - November** | **Autumn recovery.** Second buying wave, especially from relocated professionals (September start dates). | Relist stale properties. Fresh marketing push. Target expat/relocation audience. |
| **December** | Quietest month. Minimal transaction activity. | Year-end admin. Prepare January pipeline. Send owner reports. |

### Key Market Events

- **Golden Visa policy changes**: Monitor regulatory announcements — they cause demand spikes or drops in specific segments
- **Tax season (April-June)**: Some investors accelerate purchases for tax planning
- **University start (September)**: Drives rental investment demand, especially in Lisbon and Porto
- **Tourism season (June-September)**: Drives interest in AL (short-term rental) eligible properties

## 7. Boost Strategy (Paid Promotion)

### When to Use Paid Boosts

Idealista offers several paid visibility products:

| Product | Cost (approx.) | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Destaque (Highlight)** | EUR 30-80/month | Properties needing visibility in competitive areas |
| **Super Destaque** | EUR 80-150/month | High-priority listings needing top-of-search placement |
| **Homepage banner** | EUR 200-500/month | Luxury properties targeting broad awareness |

### Boost Decision Framework

**DO boost when:**
- Property is in Bucket A (Momentum) and you want to accelerate contacts
- New listing during peak season (March-June) in a competitive area
- Luxury property where the small buyer pool means every impression counts
- Property has been relisted after algorithm reset — maximize the fresh boost window

**DO NOT boost when:**
- Listing quality score is below 85% (fix the listing first, then boost)
- Property is overpriced relative to comparables (boosts amplify bad pricing into more views with no contacts)
- Low season (December-February) for entry/mid market
- Property is in Bucket B (Fixable) with unresolved photo/description issues

### ROI Calculation

```
Boost ROI = (Additional contacts from boost x Conversion rate x Average commission) / Boost cost

Example:
- Boost cost: EUR 80/month
- Additional contacts: 8 per month
- Conversion to showing: 25% = 2 showings
- Conversion to sale: 10% of showings = 0.2 sales
- Average commission: EUR 10,000
- Expected value: EUR 2,000/month
- ROI: 2,400%
```

Even modest boost spending is highly ROI-positive when the listing is optimized.

## 9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Poor photo quality** | Kills click-through rate from search results. #1 reason for low views. | Professional photographer or high-quality smartphone with proper technique. |
| **Incomplete structured fields** | Direct quality score penalty. Fields like floor, elevator, parking are used in search filters — missing = invisible to filtered searches. | Complete ALL fields before publishing. Use [TBD] in description only, never in structured fields. |
| **Generic descriptions** | Copy-paste descriptions signal low effort. Buyers disengage. | Write unique descriptions for every property using the six-part structure. |
| **Hashtag spam** | Idealista penalizes descriptions with hashtags. They add no SEO value on the platform. | Remove all hashtags. Use natural language with relevant keywords. |
| **Wrong category** | Listing a building as "apartment" or vice versa reduces visibility to the wrong audience. | Verify property type matches the actual asset. |
| **Price inconsistency** | Listing at one price on Idealista and another on Kyero creates distrust. | Sync prices across ALL channels on every update. |
| **No floor plan** | 10% quality score penalty. Buyers increasingly expect floor plans. | Commission floor plans for every property. Services like Floorplanner cost EUR 30-50. |
| **Missing energy certificate** | Legal requirement in Portugal. Idealista flags listings without it. | Obtain certificate before listing. Minimum Class F is common for older properties. |
| **Publishing before ready** | Wastes the "new listing" boost window with a subpar listing. | NEVER publish until all photos, description, floor plan, and structured fields are complete. |
| **Ignoring mobile preview** | 70%+ of Idealista traffic is mobile. Long titles get cut. First photo is crucial. | Preview every listing on mobile before publishing. |

## 10. Weekly Idealista Audit Checklist

Run this checklist every Monday:

- [ ] Check quality score for all active listings (target 85%+)
- [ ] Review contact rates vs. targets by segment
- [ ] Check view counts — declining views = algorithm deprioritization
- [ ] Verify all structured fields are still complete (Idealista sometimes resets fields after edits)
- [ ] Review competitor listings in the same area/price range
- [ ] Check for new comparable listings that change our pricing position
- [ ] Verify photo count and ordering are correct
- [ ] Respond to any unanswered contacts (target: 0 pending contacts at any time)
- [ ] Update performance log in property-portfolio.md
