# Owner Management Playbook — Realization Portugal

> How we build and maintain trust with property owners throughout the sales cycle.

---

## 1. Communication Cadence

| Frequency | Type | Content | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** | Performance update | Views, contacts, showings, pipeline activity | Email (template below) |
| **Bi-weekly** | Market commentary | Relevant market trends, comparable transactions, regulatory news | Email or brief call |
| **Monthly** | Strategy call | Full performance review, pricing analysis, action plan for next month | Video call or in-person (30 min) |
| **Event-triggered** | Showing feedback | Immediate relay of buyer feedback after every showing | Phone call (within 2 hours) |
| **Event-triggered** | Offer received | Immediate communication of any offer | Phone call (within 1 hour) |
| **Event-triggered** | Market shift | Significant comparable sale, regulatory change, or competitive listing | Email + call if urgent |

### Communication Principles

1. **Proactive, not reactive.** Owners should never have to ask us for updates. We provide them before they think to ask.
2. **Data over opinion.** Every recommendation is backed by numbers — views, contacts, comparable transactions, days on market.
3. **Honest, not optimistic.** We tell owners what the market is saying, not what they want to hear. This builds long-term trust.
4. **Consistent format.** Owners learn to expect and read our reports. Same format every time.
5. **Respect their time.** Weekly updates are concise. Monthly calls are structured with an agenda.

### Key Rules for Weekly Updates

- Always include the comparative figure from the previous week
- Keep it under 200 words — owners want clarity, not essays

## 4. Price Conversation Frameworks

### Scenario 1: "The price is firm" (e.g., Arroios at EUR 1.45M)

**Owner position**: Will not reduce. Price is non-negotiable.

**Our approach**:

1. **Respect it completely.** Never undermine the owner's price decision — it erodes trust.
2. **Reframe the strategy**: "We agree that at this price, the strategy is not about waiting for the market — it's about finding the specific buyer who sees the value. Let me explain how we'll do that."
3. **Present specialized channel strategy**:
   - "Standard portals attract standard buyers. For this property, we need to reach [architecture community / international collectors / creative professionals]."
   - Detail specific channels and outreach plan
4. **Set timeline expectations**: "At this price point, we should plan for a 6-12 month marketing cycle. Each month, we expand our reach to new audiences."
5. **Regular reporting**: Continue weekly updates. Show the owner you are actively working, not just waiting.
6. **Never say "overpriced"**: If asked directly, say "The price positions this in the ultra-premium segment, which has a smaller buyer pool. Our job is to find that buyer."

### Scenario 2: "Other agents promised a higher price / faster sale"

**Owner position**: Another agent valued the property higher or promised a quick sale.

**Our approach**:

1. **Acknowledge**: "I understand other professionals have given you different assessments. That's common in our market."
2. **Present data**: "Let me show you what the market is actually telling us."
   - Show comparable SOLD prices (not asking prices)
   - Show days on market for similar properties
   - Show the difference between asking prices and final sale prices in the area (typically 5-15% gap in Portugal)
3. **The honesty framework**: "We have two options. We can list at the higher price and test the market — but the risk is that after 3-4 months with low interest, we'll need to reduce, and by then we've lost the 'new listing' advantage. Or we can price competitively now and attract multiple interested buyers, which often results in a sale at or near asking price."
4. **Offer a compromise**: "Let's list at [slightly above our recommended price] for 8 weeks. If we get fewer than [X] contacts per week, we revisit."
5. **Never badmouth competitors**: "Every agency has their approach. Ours is based on data and transparency."

### Scenario 3: "Why hasn't it sold?"

**Owner position**: Frustrated with lack of results after extended time on market.

**Our approach**:

1. **Validate the frustration**: "I completely understand your frustration. Let me walk you through exactly what's happening and what we can do about it."
2. **Present the diagnostic**:
   - **Views are low?** → "The algorithm is deprioritizing the listing. We need to refresh it." (Propose delist/relist strategy)
   - **Views are fine but contacts are low?** → "People are seeing the listing but not engaging. This is usually a pricing or presentation issue." (Show contact rate benchmarks)
   - **Contacts are fine but no offers?** → "We're generating interest but buyers are not committing. Let me share the feedback we've received." (Share showing feedback themes)
3. **Present a concrete action plan with timeline**:
   - "Here are 3 specific things we will do in the next 2 weeks, and here's what we expect to see change."
4. **If price is the issue, say so diplomatically**:
   - "The data suggests the market values this property between EUR [X] and EUR [Y]. At EUR [current], we're above what most active buyers are prepared to pay. A repositioning to EUR [recommended] would place us in the competitive range."
5. **Offer metrics-driven checkpoints**: "Let's agree: we implement these changes, and if we don't see [specific metric improvement] in 3 weeks, we discuss further adjustments."

### Scenario 4: "Should I reduce the price?"

**Owner position**: Considering a reduction but unsure.

**Our approach**:

1. **Present evidence, not opinion**:
   - Recent comparable sold prices
   - Current competing listings (what buyer alternatives exist)
   - Our property's contact rate vs. segment benchmark
   - Days on market vs. average for the segment
2. **If data supports a reduction**:
   - "Based on comparable transactions and our contact rate, a repositioning to EUR [X] would place us competitively. Here's why I recommend this specific number: [data]."
   - Recommend meaningful reductions (5%+ for real impact) rather than token adjustments (1-2% that change nothing)
3. **If data does NOT support a reduction**:
   - "Before adjusting price, I want to exhaust our marketing optimization. The contact rate suggests [specific issue] that we can fix without a price change."
   - Present specific marketing actions first
4. **Combine with algorithm reset**: "If we reduce, I recommend we delist for 30 days and relist at the new price. This gives us a fresh 'new listing' boost and the algorithm won't penalize us for a price drop."
5. **Frame as a positive action**: "A well-timed price repositioning is not a sign of failure — it's a strategic move that signals to the market that you're a serious seller."

## 5. Contract Renewal Protocols

### Standard Contract Terms

| Element | Standard | Negotiable Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 6 months | 3-12 months |
| **Commission** | 5% + IVA | 3-5% + IVA depending on property value |
| **Exclusivity** | Preferred but not required | Exclusive or shared mediation |
| **Notice period** | 30 days before expiration | 15-30 days |

### Renewal Timeline

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| **60 days before expiry** | Internal review: is this contract worth renewing? Performance assessment. |
| **45 days before expiry** | Owner conversation: present performance summary, discuss renewal terms. |
| **30 days before expiry** | If renewing: sign new contract. If not: prepare offboarding. |
| **At renewal** | Use the opportunity to: adjust price strategy, refresh listing, renegotiate terms if needed. |

### Renewal Conversation Framework

1. Open with performance summary: "Over the last [X] months, here's what we achieved: [data]."
2. Present market context: "Here's how the market has evolved during our contract period."
3. Propose next-period strategy: "For the renewal period, here's my recommended approach."
4. Discuss terms: "Are there any adjustments to our arrangement you'd like to discuss?"
5. Confirm renewal or handle graceful exit.

## 6. Commission Structure Reference

| Property Value | Standard Commission | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **< EUR 250K** | 5% + IVA | Entry segment — higher % to justify effort on lower-value transactions |
| **EUR 250K-500K** | 4-5% + IVA | Mid segment — negotiable based on exclusivity and expected sale timeline |
| **EUR 500K-1M** | 3-5% + IVA | Luxury — may accept lower % on higher absolute value |
| **> EUR 1M** | 3-4% + IVA | Ultra-luxury — lower % justified by high absolute commission |
| **Co-broke (AMI sharing)** | 50/50 split | Standard AMI co-broke arrangement |

### IVA (VAT) Note

All commission rates are expressed before IVA. Current IVA rate on real estate mediation services in Portugal: **23%**.

Example: EUR 500K property at 4% commission = EUR 20,000 + EUR 4,600 IVA = EUR 24,600 total invoiced to owner.

## 7. Owner Onboarding Checklist (New Properties)

When signing a new property, complete the following within the first 7 days:

### Day 1-2: Documentation

- [ ] Signed mediation contract (contrato de mediação imobiliária)
- [ ] Property registration (caderneta predial) — request copy from owner
- [ ] Land registry certificate (certidão permanente) — verify ownership and encumbrances
- [ ] Energy certificate (certificado energético) — must be valid at time of listing
- [ ] Floor plans (if available)
- [ ] Building permits and licenses (if applicable — especially for buildings/renovation projects)
- [ ] Condominium minutes (if apartment — confirms no outstanding issues)
- [ ] Owner's preferred contact method and availability for showings

### Day 3-4: Property Assessment

- [ ] Physical visit and detailed property inspection
- [ ] Photography (professional photographer or scheduled within 5 days)
- [ ] Measurements verification (cross-check with caderneta predial)
- [ ] Condition assessment and any issues to disclose
- [ ] Neighborhood walk — identify selling points, transport, amenities
- [ ] Comparable market analysis (CMA) — minimum 5 comparables

### Day 5-7: Listing Preparation

- [ ] CMA and pricing recommendation presented to owner
- [ ] Agreed listing price confirmed in writing
- [ ] Description written (using appropriate segment template)
- [ ] All structured fields prepared for Idealista submission
- [ ] Photos edited, ordered, and ready for upload
- [ ] Floor plan prepared (commission if not available)
- [ ] Channel strategy defined (which portals, which marketing)
- [ ] Listing published on primary channel (Idealista)
- [ ] Cross-channel listings within 48 hours of primary
- [ ] First weekly update scheduled

## 8. Delisting / Relisting Strategy

### When to Delist

| Scenario | Recommended Action |
|---|---|
| **Listing stale (60+ days), declining views** | Delist, wait 30 days, relist with improvements |
| **Renovation pause** | Delist immediately. Relist only when renovation is complete. |
| **Seasonal timing** | Delist in December, relist in March for peak season boost |
| **Price reduction > 10%** | Delist, relist at new price (avoids "price reduced" stigma) |
| **Photo/description overhaul** | If changes are dramatic, delist/relist to get new-listing boost |
| **Owner request** | Comply immediately. Document reason. |

### Delist/Relist Protocol

1. **Before delisting**: Download all listing data, photos, and performance metrics
2. **Delist**: Remove from all channels (Idealista, Kyero, etc.) on the same day
3. **Wait period**: Minimum 30 days for Idealista algorithm reset. 14 days for other portals.
4. **During wait**: Improve all listing elements (photos, description, price if applicable)
5. **Relist**: Publish as new listing. Ensure all quality score components are maximized.
6. **Timing**: Relist on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning (highest Idealista traffic days)
7. **Boost**: Consider paid boost during the first 2 weeks to stack with new-listing boost
8. **Notify owner**: "We have relisted your property with updated marketing. Here's what changed: [list improvements]."

### Algorithm Reset Timing Guide

| Delist Duration | Algorithm Reset? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| < 14 days | Unlikely | Idealista may recognize it as the same listing |
| 14-29 days | Partial | Some portals reset, Idealista may not fully |
| 30+ days | Full reset | Recommended minimum for Idealista |
| 60+ days | Complete refresh | Best for significant repositioning |

## 9. Event-Triggered Communication

### After Every Showing

**Within 2 hours of showing completion:**

1. **Call the owner** (not email, not WhatsApp — phone call for personal touch)
2. Structure the feedback:
   - "The viewing went [well/OK/mixed]. Here's the specific feedback:"
   - What the buyer liked
   - What the buyer raised as concerns
   - The buyer's stated next steps
   - Our assessment of their seriousness (scoring)
3. **If buyer is interested**: "They've asked for [next step]. I recommend we [action]."
4. **If buyer is not interested**: "They felt [reason]. This is [common/unique] feedback. [Here's how we address it going forward / this doesn't change our strategy.]"
5. **Document everything** in the property performance log

### After Receiving an Offer

**Within 1 hour of receiving any offer:**

1. **Call the owner immediately**
2. Present the offer factually:
   - Offered price
   - Buyer profile (investor/end-user, financing/cash, timeline)
   - Any conditions (financing contingency, inspection, timeline)
3. Present market context: "This offer is [X%] below/at/above asking. Based on comparables, this is [reasonable/low/strong]."
4. **Never recommend acceptance or rejection directly**: "Here are the facts. What would you like to do?"
5. If owner wants to counter: "I recommend countering at EUR [X] because [data-driven reasoning]."
6. Document all communication in writing as follow-up to the call

### After a Deal Falls Through

1. **Call the owner within 2 hours**
2. Explain what happened factually
3. Provide clear next steps: "Here's our plan to find the next buyer."
4. Demonstrate that the pipeline is still active (other leads, upcoming showings)
5. **Do not dwell on the lost deal** — focus energy forward

## 10. Difficult Owner Situations

### Owner Wants to Switch Agencies

1. **Don't panic.** Ask: "Can you share what's driving this decision? I'd like to understand if there's something we can do differently."
2. **Listen fully.** Do not defend or argue.
3. If the issues are fixable: "I hear you. Here's a specific plan to address [concerns] in the next 2 weeks. If you don't see improvement, I completely understand."
4. If the relationship is genuinely broken: "I respect your decision. Let me ensure a smooth transition — here's all the documentation and lead history for your property."
5. **Always part professionally.** Today's departing client is tomorrow's referral.

### Owner Micromanages or Calls Excessively

1. **Set expectations early** in the onboarding: "You'll receive a detailed weekly update every Monday. Between updates, I'm always reachable for urgent matters."
2. If calls become excessive: "I want to make sure you always feel informed. Would it help if I increased our updates to twice per week?"
3. Channel their energy: "Your knowledge of the property is valuable. Could you help me by [specific task: gathering old photos, documenting renovation history, etc.]?"

### Owner Rejects All Offers

1. After 3+ rejected offers at similar levels: "We've received [X] offers between EUR [Y] and EUR [Z]. The market is consistently telling us a price range. Can we discuss what this means for our strategy?"
2. Present the cost of waiting: "Each month the property remains unsold, you incur [IMI, condominium fees, opportunity cost]. At the current pace, the holding cost over 6 months is approximately EUR [X]."
3. Offer a deadline-based approach: "Let me suggest we set a review date: if no acceptable offer comes in by [date], we revisit our price positioning together."
