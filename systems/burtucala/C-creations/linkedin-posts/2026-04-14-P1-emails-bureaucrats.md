I sent 47 emails to Portuguese government offices. Only 12 got responses.

Every response came from one of 3 formats. Here they are.

Format 1: The Formal Request. Mirror the requerimento structure. Include your NIF and process number in the subject line. One specific request per email. Open with "Exmo(a) Senhor(a)." Close formally.

Format 2: The Protocol Number Follow-Up. Without the protocol number, your email joins a generic inbox. With it, the official can find your file in seconds. Reference the original submission date. Ask for status.

Format 3: The Pre-Meeting Request. Before visiting an office, email ahead. Ask what documents are required. Propose dates. Officials prefer prepared meetings.

4 rules across all formats:

1. Write in Portuguese. Always. Even if they speak English.
2. One request per email. Multiple questions create friction.
3. Include your NIF in every email. Without it, you are nobody in the system.
4. Follow up after 10 business days. Not 3. Not 5. 10.

The format is the message.
