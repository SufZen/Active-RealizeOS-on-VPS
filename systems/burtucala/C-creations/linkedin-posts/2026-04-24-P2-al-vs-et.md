AL or ET? The answer depends on 3 things most people do not check before committing.

Alojamento Local (AL) and Empreendimento Turístico (ET) are Portugal's two main accommodation licenses. Choosing the wrong one costs 4 to 8 months and thousands of euros.

The 3 decision factors:

1. Number of units. Under 9 in the same building? Probably AL. 10+ rooms or hotel-format? ET required.

2. Services offered. Reception, daily housekeeping, and breakfast can push an AL property into ET territory, even with fewer units.

3. Municipal rules. Some municipalities classify the grey zone (5 to 9 units) differently. Your Camara decides, not the national law alone.

Key differences:
AL timeline: 2 to 4 months. Cost: 200 to 500.
ET timeline: 6 to 12 months. Cost: 3,000 to 10,000+.

The wrong choice means renovation changes, permit delays, and fines up to 40,000.
