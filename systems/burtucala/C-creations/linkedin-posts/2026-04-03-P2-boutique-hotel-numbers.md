I have reviewed 30+ boutique hotel deals in Portugal. Only 8 passed these 4 checks.

Before committing capital, run these numbers:

1. Capex per sqm. Full renovation in Portugal runs 1,200 to 1,800 per sqm. Heritage buildings: 2,500+. If total project cost per lettable sqm exceeds the area's finished property rates, you are overpaying.

2. RevPAR. Pull 12 months of comparable data, not just peak season. Lisbon boutique range: 60 to 120. Secondary cities: 40 to 80. If your model assumes 75%+ occupancy year-round, you are being optimistic.

3. Break-even months. A 6 to 10 room hotel should break even within 12 to 24 months. Beyond 30 months? Rework the deal structure.

4. Net yield after all costs. Brokers quote 6 to 8% gross. After management, IMI, maintenance, vacancy, and commissions, net is closer to 3.5 to 5.5%.

These 4 checks take 2 to 3 hours. They eliminate 60 to 70% of deals that look good on the surface.
