My contractor said 4 months. It took 9.

Not because of incompetence. Not because of a disaster. Because Portuguese construction operates on institutional time, not project time.

Every permit goes through a municipal queue. Every inspection requires scheduling across a district. Every revision resets a clock.

Across the projects we have worked on, the average timeline overrun is 60 to 90%.

3 things that actually help:

1. Plan for the delay from day one. Add 30 to 50% to every estimate.
2. Use dead time productively. Advance licensing, brand, suppliers, and staffing while construction waits.
3. Keep a weekly construction log. Photograph everything. Document every Camara communication.

The founders who handle Portuguese renovations best are not the ones who push hardest. They are the ones who planned for the wait.
