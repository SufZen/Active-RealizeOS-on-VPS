10 square meters. That is all it took to add 30% more covers to a Lisbon restaurant.

The dining room seated 60. The kitchen could produce 50 plates per service. The bottleneck was invisible from the front of house.

The chef crossed the dish zone 8 to 10 steps per plate to reach cold storage. The pantry was underused. Dirty dishes crossed the clean plating area.

3 changes. Zero walls moved. 10sqm reallocated:

1. Cold storage moved next to the prep station. 8 to 10 fewer steps per plate.
2. Underused pantry converted to dedicated cold prep zone. Main line freed for hot production.
3. Dishwashing entry relocated to a pass-through window. Clean and dirty circuits separated.

Result: kitchen output went from 50 to 65 plates per service. Covers increased from 45 to 58. HACCP compliance actually improved because circuits no longer crossed.

Cost: 1,200 for utility reconnections. 3 days of kitchen downtime.

The principle: design F&B spaces from operations backward, not aesthetics forward. Start with throughput, then design the customer-facing space to match.
