The architect's plan was stunning. The revenue model said it would lose 2,400 per month.

220sqm guesthouse in Lisbon. 6 rooms. Cafe on the ground floor. Beautiful design.

3 problems the floor plans could not show:

1. The common area was 35sqm. That is 16% of total space generating zero revenue. One extra room at 85 per night and 60% occupancy: 18,600 per year in lost revenue.

2. The cafe seated 18 but the kitchen could serve 12. HACCP compliance at 8sqm limits you to prep-only. The seating promised more than the kitchen could deliver.

3. No housekeeping storage on either floor. Beautiful closets in every room, but the operational team would carry supplies from the basement daily.

3 specific changes turned a 2,400 monthly loss into a 1,800 monthly surplus. Additional cost: 4,000. Budget stayed at 180,000.

The design remained beautiful. It just now supported a business.

Before approving architectural plans, ask your architect:
1. What is the revenue sqm ratio?
2. Does service capacity match customer capacity?
3. Where do operations happen?
