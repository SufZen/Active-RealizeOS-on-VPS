# Alojamento Local vs. Empreendimento Turístico: Which License Do You Actually Need?

**Category:** Finance/Tax/Legislation / Real Estate | **Pillar:** Venture & Investment Logic

## Term / Concept

Two Portuguese licensing categories that international investors frequently confuse: **Alojamento Local (AL)** and **Empreendimento Turístico (ET).**

Choosing the wrong one costs months and thousands of euros. Here is how to decide.

## Definition

**Alojamento Local (AL)** is Portugal's short-term rental license. It covers smaller operations: apartments, individual houses, rooms within a house, or hostels with limited capacity. AL is registered through the **RNAL** (Registo Nacional de Alojamento Local) and regulated at the municipal level.

**Empreendimento Turístico (ET)** is the broader tourism enterprise license. It covers hotels, aparthotels, resorts, tourist villages, and larger accommodation operations. ET is registered with **Turismo de Portugal** (the national tourism authority) and involves a more complex approval process including architectural project review, safety certification, accessibility compliance, and star classification.

The fundamental difference: AL is a registration (simpler, faster). ET is a license (more complex, longer, more requirements).

## Example

Consider an investor buying a 300sqm building in Setúbal to convert into an accommodation business.

**If the plan is 4 apartments for short-term rental (under 9 units in the same building):** AL is likely the correct path. Apply through the Balcão do Empreendedor. Municipal inspection. Timeline: 2 to 4 months.

**If the plan is a 12-room boutique hotel with reception, breakfast service, and daily housekeeping:** ET is required. Submit architectural project to Camara Municipal. Turismo de Portugal classification. ASAE safety inspection. Accessibility audit. Timeline: 6 to 12 months.

**The grey zone:** 5 to 9 units in one building. Some municipalities classify this as AL. Others require ET. The answer depends on your specific Camara and the services you plan to offer (reception, daily cleaning, and breakfast can push an AL property into ET territory).

## Comparison Table

| Criteria | AL | ET |
|----------|-----|-----|
| **Typical capacity** | 1 to 9 units (varies by municipality) | 10+ rooms or hotel-format operations |
| **Registration body** | RNAL (municipal level) | Turismo de Portugal (national level) |
| **Process type** | Prior communication (Mera Comunicação Prévia) | Full licensing (architectural project required) |
| **Timeline** | 2 to 4 months | 6 to 12 months |
| **Architectural project** | Not required (simple floor plan may suffice) | Required (full project by licensed architect) |
| **Star classification** | Not applicable | Required (1 to 5 stars or equivalent) |
| **Accessibility compliance** | Basic requirements | Full accessibility audit required |
| **Typical cost (licensing only)** | 200 to 500 | 3,000 to 10,000+ |
| **Operating restrictions** | Some municipalities cap or suspend new AL | Generally available but more demanding |

## Why It Matters

The wrong license choice has 3 consequences:

1. **Time.** If you prepare for AL and discover you need ET, the additional process adds 4 to 8 months. If you have already started renovation based on AL requirements, the ET architectural project may require changes.

2. **Money.** ET licensing costs 5 to 20 times more than AL. Budget 3,000 to 10,000 for ET fees, architectural project, and compliance consultants. AL costs 200 to 500 total.

3. **Legal risk.** Operating under the wrong license category is a violation. Fines for unlicensed tourism operations range from 2,500 to 40,000.

The decision depends on 3 factors: number of units, services offered, and municipal classification rules. Get this right before you sign a purchase agreement, not after.
