# AL License Step by Step: What They Don't Tell You on the Government Site

**Category:** Finance/Tax/Legislation | **Pillar:** Portugal Business OS

The **Alojamento Local** (AL) is Portugal's short-term rental license. It covers apartments, houses, rooms, and hostels rented for stays typically under 30 days. The government website lists approximately 6 steps to apply. The real process has 14.

Here is the full list, in the order that actually works.

## Stuck

If you are reading this, you have probably already visited the RNAL website (**Registo Nacional de Alojamento Local**) and found a simplified overview that skips half the steps. Or you have received conflicting advice from a lawyer, an accountant, and a property manager who each described a different process.

The confusion is structural. AL licensing is municipal-level, meaning rules differ between Lisbon, Porto, the Algarve, and smaller cities. Some municipalities have suspended new AL licenses in certain zones. Others still issue them freely. The process described here covers the general national framework. Always verify specifics with your local **Camara Municipal** (city hall).

## The 14 Steps

### Pre-Application (Steps 1-5)

**Step 1: Verify AL eligibility in your municipality.**
Before anything else, confirm that your municipality still issues AL licenses for your property type and location. Lisbon centro and parts of Porto have restricted new AL licenses in certain areas. Call or email your Camara Municipal to check. Timeline: 1 to 2 weeks for a clear answer.

**Step 2: Register your tax activity at Finanças.**
Open or update your tax activity at the **Finanças** (tax authority) to include CAE code 55201 (AL activity). If you do not have a Portuguese **NIF** (tax number) yet, get one first. If you are a non-resident, you will need a **Representante Fiscal** (fiscal representative). Timeline: 1 to 3 days if done in person; 2 to 4 weeks by mail.

**Step 3: Register with Segurança Social.**
Notify **Segurança Social** (social security) of your AL activity. This is separate from Finanças and frequently forgotten. Timeline: 1 to 2 weeks.

**Step 4: Obtain property ownership or rental documentation.**
You need proof of right-to-use: **Caderneta Predial** (property tax document from Finanças) if you own, or a rental contract with explicit permission for AL use if you rent. Many rental contracts in Portugal prohibit sub-letting. Check yours.

**Step 5: Condominium authorization (if applicable).**
If the property is in a building with a condominium, you may need authorization from the condominium assembly. This requirement varies by municipality and building regulations. Getting condominium approval can take 1 to 3 months if a general assembly vote is required.

### Application (Steps 6-9)

**Step 6: Submit the AL application on the Balcão do Empreendedor.**
The **Balcão do Empreendedor** (Entrepreneur's Desk) is the online portal for business registrations. Submit the "Mera Comunicação Prévia" (prior communication) form. Include property details, capacity, proposed AL category (apartment, house, hostel, or rooms). Timeline: 30 minutes if documents are ready.

**Step 7: Pay the application fee.**
Fees vary by municipality. Budget 50 to 150 for the application itself. Payment is typically made through the Balcão portal or at the Camara.

**Step 8: Submit the declaration of property suitability.**
A signed declaration that the property meets minimum habitability, safety, and hygiene standards. In some municipalities, this is part of the main application. In others, it is a separate submission.

**Step 9: Wait for the Camara Municipal inspection.**
The Camara has the right to inspect the property within a defined period after your application (typically 30 to 60 days). Some municipalities inspect every property. Others inspect selectively. During this period, you can technically begin operating under the "prior communication" regime, but many operators wait for the inspection to clear before accepting guests.

### Compliance Setup (Steps 10-13)

**Step 10: Install mandatory safety equipment.**
Fire extinguishers (one per floor, minimum). Fire blanket in the kitchen. Emergency evacuation plan posted visibly. Smoke detectors in all sleeping areas. First aid kit. The specific requirements depend on capacity and property type.

**Step 11: Set up the Complaints Book (Livro de Reclamações).**
The **Livro de Reclamações** (complaints book) is mandatory for all AL properties. It is available from the INCM (Imprensa Nacional - Casa da Moeda) or through their online portal. It must be physically available to guests. Cost: approximately 20.

**Step 12: Obtain property insurance.**
Civil liability insurance is mandatory for AL. Coverage should include property damage and guest liability. Annual cost: 150 to 400 depending on property size and insurer. Shop around; rates vary significantly.

**Step 13: Register with the SEF guest reporting system.**
**SEF** (Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras, now AIMA) requires all accommodation providers to report guest identities within 3 business days of check-in. This is done through an online system (Boletins de Alojamento). Registration is free but requires separate account setup.

### Go Live (Step 14)

**Step 14: Register on the RNAL and obtain your AL number.**
Once approved, your property is listed on the **RNAL** (Registo Nacional de Alojamento Local). You receive an AL registration number that must be displayed on all advertising, booking platforms, and at the property entrance.

This number is what makes your operation legal. Without it, you face fines ranging from 2,500 to 40,000 depending on the municipality and violation type.

## Tips

- **Start licensing before renovation is complete.** Steps 1 through 5 can begin while construction is still underway. This saves 2 to 3 months on total project time.
- **Keep every protocol number.** Every submission to a Portuguese government office generates a protocol number. Save them all. You will need them for follow-ups.
- **Budget 2 to 4 months total.** Fast municipalities: 2 months. Slow municipalities: 4+ months. Add 30% contingency.
- **Do not assume your lawyer knows the current rules.** AL regulations change frequently. Verify specific municipal rules directly with the Camara, not just through your legal advisor.
- **The prior communication regime lets you start early.** In most municipalities, you can begin operating after submitting the application (Step 6) without waiting for the inspection. But this carries risk: if the inspection finds non-compliance, you may need to close temporarily and remediate.

## When to Get Help

If any of the following apply, consider professional support:

- Your municipality has AL restrictions (Lisbon centro, parts of Porto)
- The property requires condominium approval
- You are a non-resident and need a fiscal representative
- The property requires a change of use (residential to commercial)
- You plan to operate as a hostel (additional regulations apply)
