# The 4 Numbers That Tell You If a Boutique Hotel Deal Works in Portugal

**Category:** Real Estate / Opportunity | **Pillar:** Venture & Investment Logic

Portugal attracts boutique hotel investors for good reason: year-round tourism demand, relatively affordable entry compared to Spain or France, and growing appetite for design-led accommodation. But "attractive market" does not equal "good deal." Most deals that look promising on a broker's one-page summary fall apart when you run 4 specific checks.

## The Challenge

International investors routinely overestimate revenue and underestimate costs. The gap between a broker's projected yield and the actual net return after Portuguese operating realities is typically 30 to 40%.

This is not because brokers lie. It is because their projections do not account for licensing timelines, renovation cost escalation, seasonal occupancy variance, and the real cost of running a hospitality operation in Portugal.

## The Principle

Before committing capital, run 4 numbers. If any one of them fails, the deal does not work. Walk away.

## The 4 Numbers

### Number 1: Capex Per Sqm (Total Project Cost)

Calculate the full renovation cost per square meter, including acquisition, construction, FF&E (furniture, fixtures, and equipment), licensing fees, soft costs (architect, engineer, permits), and a 20% contingency.

**What to check:**
- Light renovation in Portugal: 800 to 1,200 per sqm
- Full renovation (structural changes): 1,200 to 1,800 per sqm
- Heritage building conversion: 1,800 to 2,500+ per sqm

**Red flag:** If your total project cost (acquisition + renovation) divided by lettable sqm exceeds the area's going rate for comparable finished properties, you are overpaying for the build. The numbers must leave room for a return.

### Number 2: RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room)

**RevPAR** is the single most important metric for a hotel investment. It is calculated as average daily rate multiplied by occupancy rate.

**What to check:**
- Research comparable properties on booking platforms. Pull 12 months of data, not just peak season.
- Lisbon boutique hotels: RevPAR ranges from 60 to 120 depending on location and positioning.
- Secondary cities (Setúbal, Coimbra, Évora): 40 to 80 RevPAR range.
- Seasonal variance in the Algarve can mean 150+ in summer and 30 in winter.

**Red flag:** If your revenue model assumes year-round occupancy above 75%, you are being optimistic. Conservative planning uses 55 to 65% annual occupancy for year one.

### Number 3: Break-Even in Months

Calculate how many months of full operation it takes to cover all fixed and variable costs. Include: mortgage or rent, insurance, utilities, staff, marketing, platform commissions (15 to 20% on OTAs), maintenance reserve (5% of revenue), management (if not owner-operated), and IMI (property tax).

**What to check:**
- A well-structured 6 to 10 room boutique hotel in Portugal should reach operational break-even within 12 to 24 months.
- If your model shows break-even beyond 30 months, the deal structure needs rework.

**Red flag:** If break-even depends on "growing into" a higher rate or occupancy in year 2, question whether you are building assumptions on hope rather than data.

### Number 4: Net Yield After All Costs

Calculate the actual net return on total invested capital after all operating costs, taxes, and reserves.

**What to check:**
- Gross yield projections from brokers typically show 6 to 8%. These ignore management costs, IMI, condominium fees, maintenance, and vacancy.
- Realistic net yield for a boutique hotel in Portugal: 3.5 to 5.5% on total invested capital.
- Anything below 3% net does not justify the complexity and risk of a hospitality operation.

**Red flag:** If someone promises net yields above 7%, ask to see the cost model. The number is almost certainly missing major expense lines.

## The Outcome

These 4 checks take 2 to 3 hours of research. They eliminate 60 to 70% of deals that look good on the surface. The remaining 30 to 40% are worth investigating further.

The discipline is simple: run the numbers before you fall in love with the property.
