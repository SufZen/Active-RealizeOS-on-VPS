# Burtucala — Content Formats

> Entity: ENT-02 | 6 recurring formats. Each piece follows exactly one.

---

## Format 1: Reality Log

**Purpose:** Concrete field stories. First-person. One specific moment turned into an insight.

**Best for:** P1 (Energy & Efficiency), P3 (Space, Design & Brand)

**Structure:**

```
1. SCENE (2-3 sentences)
   What happened. Where. When. Set the moment.

2. TENSION (2-3 sentences)
   What went wrong, surprised you, or created friction.

3. INSIGHT (3-5 sentences)
   What you learned from it. The takeaway.

4. APPLICATION (2-3 sentences)
   What the reader can do with this insight. Practical.

5. CTA (1 sentence)
   Soft, specific. "Start with the strategy session." or "Book a Fit Call."
```

**Length:** Blog: 800-1,200 words. LinkedIn: 150-400 words (condensed).

**Example opening:** "Last Tuesday, I walked into the Camara Municipal expecting my license application to take 20 minutes. I left 3 hours later with a new understanding of how Portuguese municipal offices actually work."

## Format 2: Bureaucracy Journal

**Purpose:** Navigating Portuguese processes. Step-by-step with honest commentary.

**Best for:** P4 (Portugal Business OS), P1 (Energy & Efficiency)

**Structure:**

```
1. STUCK (2-3 sentences)
   The frustration. What the reader is probably dealing with.

2. STEPS (numbered list)
   What we actually did to get through it. Specific, ordered.

3. TIPS (bulleted list)
   What saves time. What to avoid. What we wish we'd known.

4. WHEN TO GET HELP (2-3 sentences)
   The point where DIY stops making sense. Who to call.
```

**Length:** Blog: 800-1,500 words. LinkedIn: 200-500 words (top 3 tips only).

**Example opening:** "The AL license application has 14 steps. The government website lists 6. Here is the full list, in the order that actually works."

## Format 3: Portugal Business Dictionary

**Purpose:** One concept, explained clearly. Reference content.

**Best for:** P4 (Portugal Business OS), P2 (Venture & Investment Logic)

**Structure:**

```
1. TERM / CONCEPT (1 line)
   The word or phrase being explained.

2. DEFINITION (2-3 sentences)
   Plain English. What it actually means.

3. EXAMPLE (3-5 sentences)
   Real context. How this shows up in practice.

4. WHY IT MATTERS (2-3 sentences)
   Business impact. Why the reader should care.
```

**Length:** Blog: 400-800 words. LinkedIn: 150-300 words.

**Example opening:** "Fiscalizacao. You will hear this word on every construction project in Portugal. Here is what it means and why it matters for your budget."

## Format 4: Operator Playbook

**Purpose:** Systems and best practices. How we run things. Reusable processes.

**Best for:** P1 (Energy & Efficiency), P3 (Space, Design & Brand)

**Structure:**

```
1. CHALLENGE (2-3 sentences)
   The problem or recurring situation.

2. PRINCIPLE (1-2 sentences)
   The approach or framework we use.

3. STEPS (numbered list)
   The process. How to implement the principle.

4. OUTCOME (2-3 sentences)
   What this produces when done right.
```

**Length:** Blog: 800-1,500 words. LinkedIn: 200-500 words.

**Example opening:** "Every venture-build project we run uses the same 4-sprint structure. Sprint 0 is the diagnostic. Here is how it works and why we never skip it."

---

## Format 5: Case Files

**Purpose:** Deep anonymized project stories. Longer form. Showcases methodology.

**Best for:** P2 (Venture & Investment Logic), P3 (Space, Design & Brand)

**Structure:**

```
1. STARTING POINT (2-3 paragraphs)
   What the client walked in with. Their situation, goals, constraints.
   (Anonymized: no names, no exact locations, details changed for privacy.)

2. DIAGNOSTIC (2-3 paragraphs)
   What we found when we dug in. The real picture vs. the assumed picture.

3. DECISION (2-3 paragraphs)
   What we recommended and why. What alternatives we considered.

4. OUTCOME (2-3 paragraphs)
   What happened. Numbers where possible. Timeline.

5. LESSONS (bulleted list)
   What anyone can take from this. Transferable insights.
```

**Length:** Blog: 1,200-1,500 words. Not suitable for LinkedIn (too long). Summarize for newsletter.

**Example opening:** "A first-time investor came to us with a 200sqm space in a Lisbon neighborhood that was 'about to explode.' They wanted a boutique hotel. Here is what the numbers actually showed."

## Format 6: Syndicate Briefs

**Purpose:** Deal underwriting summaries. Numbers-heavy. For Syndicate members and Golden Opportunities.

**Best for:** P2 (Venture & Investment Logic)

**Structure:**

```
1. THESIS (2-3 sentences)
   Why this deal. The opportunity in one statement.

2. COMPS (table or list)
   Comparable operations in the area. What they charge. Occupancy/utilization.

3. LICENSING (paragraph + timeline)
   Required licenses. Estimated timeline. Known bottlenecks.

4. CAPEX (table)
   Cost structure: acquisition, renovation, FF&E, soft costs, contingency.
   Low / Mid / High ranges.

5. WHAT MUST BE TRUE (numbered list)
   Key assumptions. If any of these are wrong, the deal does not work.

6. DISCLAIMER (standard text)
   Investment disclaimer. Always included.
```

**Length:** 3-5 pages (full dossier, produced by Deal Analyst). Summarized for blog/newsletter.

**Note:** Syndicate Briefs are primarily produced by the Deal Analyst. Writer may format or adapt narratives for publication.

## Format Selection Guide

| Content Need | Best Format |
|-------------|-------------|
| Personal field story | Reality Log |
| How-to for Portuguese processes | Bureaucracy Journal |
| Explain one concept | Portugal Business Dictionary |
| Share a system or process | Operator Playbook |
| Deep project story | Case Files |
| Deal analysis for publication | Syndicate Briefs |
