# Burtucala — Licensing Pathways in Portugal

> Entity: ENT-02 | AL, hospitality, F&B, cowork, and related licensing.

---

## Important Note

Licensing information changes frequently. All timelines and requirements should be verified against current regulations before advising clients. Flag any data older than 6 months.

---

## Alojamento Local (AL) — Short-Term Rental License

**What it covers:** Apartments, houses, rooms, and hostels rented for short stays (typically under 30 days).

**Key requirements:**
- Property registration with local Camara Municipal
- Compliance with safety and habitability standards
- Complaints book (Livro de Reclamacoes)
- Fire safety equipment
- Property insurance
- SEF (immigration) guest registration system

**Typical timeline:** 2-4 months from application to approval (varies significantly by municipality).

**Known bottlenecks:**
- Some municipalities (Lisbon centro, parts of Porto) have suspended new AL licenses in certain areas
- Municipal inspection scheduling can add 4-8 weeks
- Building condominium approval may be required in apartment buildings

**Note:** AL regulations are municipal-level. Rules differ between Lisbon, Porto, Algarve, and other regions. Always verify the specific Camara's current rules.

## Hospitality License (Hotels, Guesthouses, Boutique Hotels)

**What it covers:** Larger accommodation operations that don't qualify as AL.

**Key requirements:**
- Turismo de Portugal registration
- Detailed architectural project approved by local Camara
- Health and safety compliance (ASAE)
- Fire safety certification
- Accessibility compliance
- Professional liability insurance

**Typical timeline:** 6-12 months (project approval + construction + final inspection).

**Known bottlenecks:**
- Architectural project approval can take 3-6 months
- ASAE inspections must be scheduled after construction
- Accessibility requirements can require significant renovation work
- Classification process (stars/categories) is separate

---

## F&B License (Restaurants, Cafes, Bars, Bakeries)

**What it covers:** Food and beverage establishments.

**Key requirements:**
- Camara Municipal operating license
- ASAE food safety compliance
- HACCP plan implementation
- Kitchen ventilation and extraction system approval
- Noise assessment (if applicable)
- Terrace permit (if applicable, separate process)

**Typical timeline:** 3-6 months from application to opening.

**Known bottlenecks:**
- Kitchen extraction/ventilation can require building permits
- Noise complaints from neighbors can delay or block approval
- Terrace permits are a separate process and increasingly restricted in Lisbon

---

## Coworking / Flex Workspace

**What it covers:** Shared office and workspace environments.

**Key requirements:**
- Commercial use license from Camara Municipal
- Fire safety compliance
- Building safety certification
- Accessibility compliance
- Activity registration (CAE code)

**Typical timeline:** 2-4 months (simpler than hospitality/F&B if space is already commercial-use).

**Known bottlenecks:**
- Converting residential to commercial use requires urbanistic approval (can add 6+ months)
- Some buildings have condominium restrictions on commercial activity
- Parking requirements may apply depending on sqm

---

## Wellness / Gym / Spa

**What it covers:** Fitness studios, boutique gyms, wellness centers, spas.

**Key requirements:**
- Commercial operating license
- Health authority registration (DGS for spa/wellness)
- Safety and hygiene compliance
- Professional qualification requirements for certain services
- Water quality testing (pools, hydrotherapy)

**Typical timeline:** 3-6 months.

**Known bottlenecks:**
- Spa/water-based services have stricter health requirements
- Professional qualification verification for practitioners
- Noise and vibration standards for gyms in mixed-use buildings

---

## Mixed-Use Considerations

Many Burtucala ventures combine multiple uses (e.g., guesthouse + cafe, cowork + events).

**Key principle:** Each use requires its own license. A building with a cafe on the ground floor and rooms above needs both an F&B license and a hospitality license.

**Strategy:** Plan licensing sequentially. Start with the primary use, add secondary uses once the first is approved.

---

## General Licensing Tips

1. **Start early.** Licensing is always on the critical path. Begin before design is finalized.
2. **Talk to the Camara.** A pre-application meeting can save months. Ask what they want before you submit.
3. **Use local professionals.** Portuguese architects and engineers know the system. Foreign credentials need validation.
4. **Budget for delays.** Add 30-50% to any estimated timeline.
5. **Track municipal changes.** Regulations change. What was approved last year may not be approved today.
6. **Keep records.** Every submission, every email, every protocol number. You will need them.
