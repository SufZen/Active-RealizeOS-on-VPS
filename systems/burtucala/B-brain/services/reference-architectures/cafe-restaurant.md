# Reference Architecture: Cafe / Restaurant

> Entity: ENT-02 | 8-domain venture architecture template for F&B ventures.
> **Status:** Reference example. Adapt to specific project. All numbers are illustrative ranges.

---

## 1. Offer

**What it sells:** Food, beverages, and a third-place experience.

**Pricing model:**
- Average ticket: €8-15 (cafe), €18-35 (casual dining), €35-60+ (fine dining)
- Revenue per cover per day: ticket x turns
- Ancillary: takeaway, catering, events, merchandise

**Target customer:** Local residents + international community + tourists. Balance depends on location and concept.

---

## 2. Space

**Typical venue:** 60-150 sqm. 25-60 covers.

| Zone | Sqm Allocation | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Dining area (indoor) | 40-50% | 1.2-1.5 sqm per cover minimum |
| Kitchen | 20-30% | HACCP compliant. Extraction system is critical. |
| Bar / counter | 5-10% | Service point, display, prep |
| Storage (dry + cold) | 5-8% | Often underallocated |
| Terrace (if applicable) | Variable | Requires separate permit. Major revenue driver. |
| Staff / WC / back of house | 10-15% | Staff changing area, customer bathrooms |

**Key space decisions:**
- Open kitchen vs. closed (guest experience vs. operational flexibility)
- Terrace viability (permit, noise, seasonal use)
- Counter service vs. table service (changes staffing model)

---

## 3. Revenue

**Revenue model (80sqm cafe, 30 indoor covers, Setúbal area):**

| Scenario | Daily Covers | Avg Ticket | Monthly Revenue |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Conservative | 40 (1.3 turns) | €10 | ~€10K |
| Base | 55 (1.8 turns) | €12 | ~€17K |
| Optimistic | 75 (2.5 turns) | €14 | ~€27K |

**Break-even estimate:** Monthly fixed costs €5K-9K (rent, staffing, utilities, supplies). Break-even at ~40-55 daily covers.

**Key assumptions:**
- Food cost: 28-35% of revenue (target 30%)
- Beverage cost: 18-25% of revenue (higher margin)
- Seasonality: 20-40% variation between peak and low months depending on tourist dependency
- Terrace can add 30-50% capacity in good weather months

---

## 4. Licensing

**Required licenses:**
- **F&B operating license** (Camara Municipal) — 3-6 months
- **HACCP plan** — must be in place before opening
- **Kitchen extraction/ventilation approval** — often requires building permit
- **Terrace permit** (if applicable) — separate process, 2-4 months
- **Music/entertainment license** (if applicable)
- **Alcohol license** (included in F&B license in most cases)

**Licensing sequence:** Camara Municipal application → construction/renovation → HACCP implementation → inspection → license → open.

**Critical bottleneck:** Kitchen extraction. If the building doesn't have an existing extraction path, creating one can require building permits and neighbor approval.

**See:** `B-brain/portugal-market/licensing-pathways.md` for full details.

## 6. Operations

**Core daily processes:**
- Opening prep (kitchen prep, coffee machine warm-up, terrace setup)
- Service (ordering, kitchen production, table service, POS)
- Inventory management (daily stock check, supplier ordering)
- Closing (cleaning, cash reconciliation, prep for next day)
- Quality control (food safety, hygiene, customer satisfaction)

**Staffing (80sqm cafe, 30 covers):**

| Role | Hours/week | Monthly Cost | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Chef / Head Cook | 40-48h | €1,200-1,800 | Core team member |
| Service staff | 30-40h | €800-1,200 | Can be part-time during off-peak |
| Kitchen assistant | 20-30h | €700-900 | Prep and dishwashing |
| Owner/Manager | Variable | — | Often the operator in small F&B |

**Key operational decisions:**
- Hours of operation (breakfast + lunch, lunch + dinner, all day)
- Menu complexity (fewer items = lower waste, faster service)
- Supplier relationships (fresh market daily vs. wholesale weekly)
- Delivery/takeaway (additional revenue but operational complexity)

---

## 7. Team

**Minimum viable team:** Owner-chef + 1 service staff = 2 people.
**Full team (60+ covers):** Chef + sous chef + 2 service + 1 dishwasher = 5 people.

**Hiring notes:**
- F&B has high turnover in Portugal. Plan for recruitment costs.
- Sunday/holiday work requires legal premium (50-100%)
- Employee total cost = salary x 1.35

---

## 8. Brand

**Positioning options:**
- **Specialty concept:** Single focus (specialty coffee, sourdough bakery, natural wine)
- **Community hub:** Neighborhood gathering place, cowork-friendly, events
- **Destination dining:** Chef-driven, reservations, experience-focused
- **Fast-casual:** Counter service, high-turnover, delivery-optimized

**Brand deliverables (Sprint 1):**
- Name and positioning statement
- Menu concept (categories, price range, hero items)
- Target customer profile (local ratio vs. tourist ratio)
- Visual identity direction
