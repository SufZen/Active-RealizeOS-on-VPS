# Reference Architecture: Boutique Hotel / Guesthouse

> Entity: ENT-02 | 8-domain venture architecture template for hospitality ventures.
> **Status:** Reference example. Adapt to specific project. All numbers are illustrative ranges.

---

## 1. Offer

**What it sells:** Accommodation + experience for short-to-medium stays (2-7 nights average).

**Pricing model:**
- Room rates: ADR target €80-180 depending on location, season, and positioning
- Revenue per available room (RevPAR): target 60-75% of ADR at stabilized occupancy
- Ancillary: breakfast (included or €10-15), minibar, experiences/tours, late checkout

**Target guest:** International traveler (35-55), experience-seeking, values design and authenticity over chain hotel amenities. Increasingly: digital nomads booking 1-2 week stays.

---

## 2. Space

**Typical building:** 250-500 sqm. 6-15 rooms. 1-3 floors.

| Zone | Sqm Allocation | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Guest rooms (incl. bathrooms) | 55-65% | 18-25 sqm per room (incl. bathroom) |
| Reception / lobby / lounge | 8-12% | Can double as coworking area in smaller properties |
| Breakfast / common dining | 8-12% | 1.5 sqm per cover minimum |
| Kitchen (prep only or full) | 3-5% | HACCP compliant if serving food |
| Back of house (storage, laundry, staff) | 8-12% | Often underallocated. Plan generously. |
| Corridors / circulation | 10-15% | Building code and accessibility requirements |

**Key space decisions:**
- Reception-free check-in reduces staffing and space requirement
- Laundry in-house vs. outsourced changes space and cost significantly
- Rooftop or terrace = premium pricing but licensing and safety requirements

---

## 3. Revenue

**Revenue model (10-room guesthouse, ADR €120, Setúbal area):**

| Scenario | Occupancy | Annual Revenue | Monthly Revenue |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Conservative | 55% | ~€240K | ~€20K |
| Base | 68% | ~€298K | ~€25K |
| Optimistic | 80% | ~€350K | ~€29K |

**Break-even estimate:** Monthly fixed costs €8K-14K (staffing, insurance, utilities, maintenance, platform commissions). Break-even at ~45-55% occupancy depending on cost structure.

**Key assumptions to validate:**
- ADR based on comp set (check Booking.com, Airbnb for comparable properties)
- Occupancy ramp: expect 40-50% in year 1, stabilizing by year 2
- Platform commission: 15-20% on OTA bookings, 0% on direct bookings

---

## 4. Licensing

**Required licenses:**
- **Hospitality license** (Turismo de Portugal registration) — 6-12 months
- **Building permit** for renovation (Camara Municipal) — 3-6 months
- **Fire safety certification** — after construction
- **ASAE inspection** (health and safety) — after construction
- **Accessibility compliance** — required for new hospitality projects

**Licensing sequence:** Building permit → construction → fire safety → ASAE → Turismo de Portugal registration → open.

**See:** `B-brain/portugal-market/licensing-pathways.md` for full details.

## 6. Operations

**Core daily processes:**
- Guest check-in / check-out (automated or manual)
- Room turnover (cleaning, linen, restocking)
- Breakfast service (if included)
- Maintenance response
- Guest communication (pre-arrival, during, post-stay review request)

**Staffing (10-room, mid-range):**

| Role | Hours/week | Monthly Cost | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Manager / host | 40h | €1,400-1,800 | Can be owner-operator |
| Housekeeping | 20-30h | €700-1,000 | Part-time, scaled to occupancy |
| Breakfast / service | 15-20h | €500-700 | Part-time if breakfast included |
| Maintenance | As needed | €200-400 | Outsourced or shared |

**Key operational decisions:**
- Self check-in vs. staffed reception
- Laundry in-house vs. outsourced
- Breakfast included vs. recommended nearby
- OTA-heavy vs. direct booking strategy

---

## 7. Team

**Minimum viable team:** 1 manager + 1 part-time housekeeper = 2 people.
**Full team (10+ rooms):** Manager + 2 housekeepers + 1 breakfast/service = 4 people.

**Hiring notes:**
- Portuguese labor law requires written contracts and social security registration
- Seasonal contracts (contrato a termo) are common for tourism businesses
- Employee total cost = salary x 1.35 (employer social security + insurance)

---

## 8. Brand

**Positioning options:**
- **Design boutique:** Premium pricing, Instagram-worthy, design-forward
- **Heritage charm:** Historic building, local character, story-driven
- **Operator efficient:** Clean, functional, self-service, competitive pricing
- **Wellness-integrated:** Yoga/spa element, retreat potential, longer stays

**Brand deliverables (Sprint 1):**
- Name and positioning statement
- Target guest profile
- Experience promise (what makes this different)
- Visual direction (mood board level)
