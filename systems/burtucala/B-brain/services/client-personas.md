# Burtucala — Client Personas

> Entity: ENT-02 | Who Burtucala serves. Three primary profiles.

---

## Persona 1: The Investor-Owner

**Profile:**
- International investor (often Israeli, British, American, German, Dutch)
- Age: 35-55
- Has capital (150K-2M range) and wants to deploy it in Portugal
- May or may not want to operate the venture personally
- Often has business experience but NOT in hospitality/F&B/real estate development

**Situation:**
- Found a property or concept they like
- Uncertain about licensing, renovation costs, and operational complexity in Portugal
- Has received conflicting advice from brokers, lawyers, and friends
- Worried about making a costly mistake in an unfamiliar market

**What They Need:**
- Honest assessment of whether their idea works (Sprint 0)
- Clear numbers: capex, opex, break-even, timeline
- Licensing clarity: what is required, how long, how complex
- Trusted partner introductions (legal, tax, architecture, construction)
- A plan they can follow or delegate

**Burtucala Service Fit:**
- Entry: Fit Call > Strategy Meeting > Consultation (Service 1)
- Deeper: Syndicate membership (Service 2) for deal flow
- Build: Venture-Build DFY (Service 3B) for turnkey delivery

**Communication Style:**
- Wants confidence and clarity, not sales pressure
- Values numbers over adjectives
- Responds to constraint-first framing ("here's what limits you, here's what's possible")
- Appreciates honesty about what won't work

## Persona 2: The Operator-Founder

**Profile:**
- Entrepreneur who wants to build and run a space-based venture
- Age: 28-45
- Has operational experience (hospitality, F&B, cowork, wellness, or adjacent)
- May be relocating to Portugal or already living there
- Budget is tighter. Needs to make every euro count.

**Situation:**
- Has a concept and energy, needs structure and local knowledge
- Understands operations but doesn't know Portuguese bureaucracy
- Needs help with the "boring" parts: licensing, capex planning, supplier sourcing
- Wants a partner who works alongside them, not someone who hands them a PDF

**What They Need:**
- Sprint-based execution support (Sprint 0-3)
- Someone who has done this before and can anticipate problems
- Space program design (layout drives revenue)
- Licensing navigation (step-by-step, not theoretical)
- Operational SOPs they can use on day one

**Burtucala Service Fit:**
- Entry: Fit Call > Strategy Meeting
- Core: Venture-Build DWY (Service 3A) sprint by sprint
- Post-launch: Run Support + Advisory Board (Service 4)

**Communication Style:**
- Wants action, not theory
- Appreciates direct feedback ("this won't work because...")
- Values sprint deliverables they can use immediately
- Responds to operator language ("in the field," "what we've seen")

---

## Persona 3: The Portfolio Manager

**Profile:**
- Experienced investor managing multiple properties or ventures
- Age: 40-60
- Has existing portfolio in Portugal or other markets
- Looking for deal flow, market intelligence, and strategic advisory
- Sophisticated: understands risk, yield, and capital allocation

**Situation:**
- Doesn't need hand-holding on basics
- Wants curated opportunities that are pre-screened
- Needs ongoing strategic support for existing operations
- Values time efficiency: wants analysis delivered, not meetings

**What They Need:**
- Syndicate deal flow (pre-screened, DD-ready dossiers)
- Market intelligence (licensing changes, new areas, demand shifts)
- Advisory board relationship for strategic decisions
- Quick assessments on new opportunities

**Burtucala Service Fit:**
- Core: Syndicate Membership (Service 2)
- Ongoing: Advisory Board (Service 4) at Growth or Scale tier
- Future: Investment Fund (Service 5) when launched

**Communication Style:**
- Expects professional, concise communication
- Values density: data-rich, narrative-light
- Wants dossiers they can share with partners or advisors
- Appreciates direct access to Burtucala's principals

## Common Traits Across All Personas

- International background (not Portuguese-born)
- English as primary communication language
- Seeking clarity in a complex market
- Skeptical of hype and promises
- Value trust built through transparency and competence
- Need structure and process, not just ideas
