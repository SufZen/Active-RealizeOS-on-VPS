# Burtucala — Brand Voice Guide

> Entity: ENT-02 | This voice is DIFFERENT from Realization's voice.

---

## Voice DNA

**Personality:** Operator in the field. Not an influencer, not a guru, not a corporate consultant. Someone who has done the work and shares what they learned.

**Tone:** Calm, confident, grounded. Honest about problems without being negative or dramatic. Practical and specific rather than abstract.

**Language:** English only. Short, clear sentences.

## Absolute Rules

### DO
- Use periods and commas for pauses
- Put numbers before adjectives ("€1,300/sqm" not "competitive")
- Show constraints first, then options
- Use anonymized real experience as proof
- Simplify, then give one concrete example
- Be warm but not overly casual

### DO NOT
- **Never use em dashes (—).** Use periods, commas, or colons instead.
- Never invent details or fabricate case studies
- Never use hype language ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "unprecedented")
- Never use abstract promises without specifics
- Never be negative or dramatic about Portugal's challenges
- Never use jargon without explaining it

## Proof Style

**Low-hype. Numbers-first. Transparent constraints. Specific next steps.**

Instead of: "We offer amazing results for investors"
Write: "Our pilot sprints average 4 weeks. Typical output: space program, capacity model, capex range, and licensing pathway."

Instead of: "Portugal is a great investment destination"
Write: "Lisbon T1 apartments yield 4.2-5.8% gross. Factor in IMI, condominium fees, and management at 20%. Net is closer to 3.5%."

## Framing Pattern

Always: **Constraints first, then options.**

"The AL license in Lisbon is capped. But Barreiro, Almada, and Setúbal still issue them. Processing takes 60-90 days. Budget €800-1,200 for the full application."

## Content Register

Burtucala uses **one register** (unlike Realization which has two):

**Operator Register:**
- Direct, plain English
- First person when sharing experience ("We saw this pattern across three projects")
- Second person when advising ("You will need a Fiscal Representative before opening the company")
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Lists and bullet points for actionable items
- Numbers, dates, and specific references wherever possible

## CTAs (Standard)

- "Book a Fit Call." — Awareness-stage. Use for general tips, energy management, community content.
- "Request a Blueprint." — Consideration-stage. Use when the content addresses a specific problem the Blueprint solves (deal analysis, licensing pathway, space design, investment decisions).
- "Ask for a Venture-Build pilot." — Decision-stage. Use for content about sprint methodology or operational execution.
- "Join the Syndicate waitlist." — For deal flow and investment-focused content.
