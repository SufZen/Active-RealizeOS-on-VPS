# Marvelous Creations — Operational Standards

> Entity: ENT-05 | Zero Budget Creep. Radical Transparency. Separation of Powers.

---

## The Three Iron Principles (Expanded)

### Iron Principle 1: Zero Budget Creep

**Rule:** No expenditure is committed without a corresponding budget line and prior approval.

**How it works:**
- Every expense must map to a line in the Master Excel (Interactive Business Plan v4)
- Roy Hadar is the sole editor of the Master Excel
- Before committing any expenditure:
  1. Check if a budget line exists
  2. Confirm remaining budget in that line
  3. Get approval from the appropriate tier (see Governance Matrix)
- If no budget line exists, a budget amendment must be approved before the expense is committed
- Variance > 2.5% of total budget triggers mandatory LP notification

**Who enforces:** Roy Hadar (Head of Economics & Financial Control)

**Consequence of violation:** Any unbudgeted expense must be reported to LP within 48 hours with explanation and remediation plan.

---

### Iron Principle 2: Radical Transparency

**Rule:** All project information is accessible to all stakeholders at all times.

**How it works:**
- **Google Drive Data Room:** Organized in 4 folders (01_Legal, 02_Financial, 03_Technical, 04_Marketing). All documents filed within 24 hours of creation.
- **ClickUp:** 3 Spaces (Planning & Permitting, Finance & Legal, Sales & CRM). All tasks tracked with status, owner, and deadlines.
- **Financial data:** Monthly P&L and budget vs. actual available to LP.
- **Communication:** GP Weekly Sync notes shared. Monthly LP Update with full financial review.

**Who enforces:** All GP team members. Each is responsible for transparency in their pillar.

**Consequence of violation:** Information gaps are flagged in the next GP Weekly Sync and resolved within 48 hours.

---

### Iron Principle 3: Separation of Powers

**Rule:** The person who commits expenditure cannot be the same person who approves it.

**How it works:**

| Action | Executor | Approver |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Signing a contractor agreement | Asaf (Project Director) | Roy (Budget check) |
| Processing a payment | Roy (Treasury) | Asaf (Technical validation) |
| Approving a sales CPCV | Meirav (Sales) | Asaf (Strategic) + Roy (Financial) |
| Major procurement (> 10K) | Asaf or Meirav | Eldad (LP approval) |

**Who enforces:** The GP team collectively. Roy has veto power on any expenditure that violates budget discipline.

**Consequence of violation:** Any expenditure approved without proper separation is flagged, reviewed in the next GP Sync, and a corrective process is implemented.

## Governance Matrix (Joint Decision Axis)

| Decision Size | Process |
|--------------|---------|
| < 5,000 | Internal GP approval (Asaf or Roy unilateral) |
| 5,000 - 10,000 | Joint GP decision (Asaf + Roy both approve) |
| > 10,000 | LP approval required (Eldad / Zodiaco) |
| > 2.5% total budget variance | Mandatory LP notification (regardless of amount) |

## Communication Standards

### Internal (GP Team)
- Professional, concise, action-oriented
- Every communication should be clear about: what decision is needed, by whom, by when
- Use ClickUp for task tracking, WhatsApp for urgent coordination
- GP Weekly Sync: every Monday, standing agenda

### External (LP / Investors)
- Monthly management report (SOP 3)
- Professional, transparent, no surprises
- Plan vs. Actual format with variance explanation
- Quarterly Zoom review call with LP

### External (Partners / Contractors)
- Clear scope documentation before engagement
- Written agreements for all engagements
- Payment terms defined upfront
- Performance tracked against deliverables
