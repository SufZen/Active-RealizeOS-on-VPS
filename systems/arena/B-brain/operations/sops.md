# Arena Habitat — Standard Operating Procedures

> Entity: ENT-05 | 3 core SOPs: Procurement, Payment Cycle, Investor Reporting.

---

## SOP 1: Procurement

**Purpose:** Ensure all vendor engagements follow a structured, governance-compliant process.

### Trigger
A need for an external product or service is identified by any GP member.

### Process

```
1. IDENTIFY NEED
   Who: Any GP member
   What: Document what is needed, why, and estimated budget
   Output: Need statement with budget estimate

2. CHECK BUDGET
   Who: Roy (Finance Controller)
   What: Confirm budget line exists and has sufficient funds
   Output: Budget confirmation or flag for amendment

3. SOURCE VENDORS
   Who: GP member who owns the domain (see external-partners.md)
   What: Identify 2-3 potential vendors, collect quotes
   Output: Vendor comparison (scope, price, timeline, references)

4. EVALUATE & SELECT
   Who: Per governance matrix (< 5K: unilateral / 5-10K: joint GP / > 10K: LP)
   What: Compare vendors against criteria, select preferred
   Output: Selection decision with reasoning

5. LEGAL REVIEW
   Who: Adv. Tania Duarte (if engagement > 5K or involves contractual terms)
   What: Review agreement terms, risk assessment
   Output: Legal clearance

6. EXECUTE AGREEMENT
   Who: GP member who owns the domain
   What: Sign agreement, communicate scope and timeline to vendor
   Output: Signed agreement filed in Google Drive (01_Legal)

7. ONBOARD VENDOR
   Who: GP member who owns the domain
   What: Set up communication channels, share project context, define reporting
   Output: Vendor integrated into project workflow
```

### Timeline
- Standard: 2-4 weeks from need identification to agreement
- Expedited (< 5K, urgent): 1 week
- Major engagement (> 10K with LP approval): 3-6 weeks

## SOP 2: Payment Cycle

**Purpose:** Ensure every payment is validated, approved, and documented before funds leave the account.

### Trigger
An invoice is received from a vendor, contractor, or service provider.

### Process

```
1. RECEIVE INVOICE
   Who: GP member who manages the vendor relationship
   What: Invoice received via email or post
   Action: Forward to Roy within 24 hours

2. VALIDATE INVOICE
   Who: Roy (Finance Controller)
   What: Check invoice against:
   - Scope of work / purchase order
   - Budget line and remaining balance
   - Correct amounts, VAT, payment terms
   - Previous payments on this engagement
   Output: Validation result (approved / query / rejected)

3. TECHNICAL CONFIRMATION
   Who: Asaf (Project Director) or Architect (for design-related work)
   What: Confirm work was completed as invoiced
   Note: For construction invoices, Fiscalizacao sign-off is required
   Output: Technical approval

4. FISCALIZACAO SIGN-OFF (Construction Only)
   Who: Fiscalizacao (external)
   What: Independent verification that construction work matches invoice
   Output: Signed certification

5. GOVERNANCE APPROVAL
   Who: Per Joint Decision Axis
   - < 5K: Roy approves
   - 5-10K: Roy + Asaf approve
   - > 10K: LP approval required
   Output: Written approval

6. PROCESS PAYMENT
   Who: Roy (Finance Controller)
   What: Execute payment via NovoBanco
   When: Within 3 business days of final approval
   Output: Payment confirmation

7. FILE & RECORD
   Who: Roy
   What: File receipt in Google Drive (02_Financial)
   Record in accounting system (Audax receives documentation)
   Update Master Excel with actual spend
   Output: Updated financial records
```

### Payment Methods
- Bank transfer (NovoBanco): Standard for all payments > 500
- MB Way: Small payments (< 500)
- Check: Avoided except where specifically required

### Timeline
- Standard invoice processing: 5-7 business days
- With Fiscalizacao sign-off: 7-10 business days
- With LP approval: 10-15 business days

## SOP 3: Monthly Investor Reporting

**Purpose:** Deliver a comprehensive, transparent monthly update to the LP.

### Trigger
Month-end close (last business day of each month).

### Process

```
1. CLOSE THE MONTH (Day 1-3 of new month)
   Who: Roy
   What:
   - Finalize all transactions for the previous month
   - Reconcile bank statements
   - Update Master Excel
   - Calculate budget vs. actual variance
   Output: Monthly financial close

2. COLLECT INPUTS (Day 3-5)
   Who: All GP members
   What:
   - Asaf: Technical progress update (milestones, risks, next steps)
   - Meirav: Sales pipeline update (units status, buyer activity)
   - Roy: Financial summary (P&L, cash position, variance)
   Output: Input sections for report

3. COMPILE REPORT (Day 5-7)
   Who: Roy
   What: Assemble Monthly Management Report using template
   (see investor-relations.md for template)
   Include: Executive summary, financials, progress, sales, risks, decisions needed
   Output: Draft PDF report

4. INTERNAL REVIEW (Day 7-8)
   Who: Asaf reviews and approves final version
   What: Verify accuracy, completeness, and tone
   Output: Approved report

5. SEND TO LP (Day 8-10)
   Who: Meirav (Capital & Sales)
   What: Send report via email to Eldad
   Include: Report PDF + any supporting documentation
   Schedule: Monthly Zoom review call (within 5 business days)
   Output: Report delivered, call scheduled

6. LP REVIEW CALL (Day 10-15)
   Who: GP team + Eldad
   What: Walk through report, answer questions, discuss decisions
   Duration: 45-60 minutes
   Output: Action items documented, decisions recorded

7. LOG & FILE
   Who: Meirav (notes), Roy (financial records)
   What: File report in Google Drive (02_Financial/Monthly Reports)
   Log decisions in I-insights/decisions.md
   Output: Archived report + documented decisions
```

### Timeline
- Target: Report sent by 10th of following month
- LP call: By 15th of following month

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] All financial figures reconciled against bank statements
- [ ] Variance explained (not just reported)
- [ ] Technical progress matches reality (not aspirational)
- [ ] Sales data current
- [ ] Risks are specific (not generic)
- [ ] Decisions needed are clearly stated
- [ ] Tone is professional and transparent
