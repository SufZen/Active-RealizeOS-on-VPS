## Addressing Each Concern

### 1. "What happens in case of dissolution?"

We're defining **exit terms by project phase** in the pacto parassocial:

| Phase | What happens |
|-------|-------------|
| Before land purchase | Either of us can exit. Capital returned proportionally. |
| After land, before construction | If I (GP) exit — you keep the land entirely. If you (LP) exit — you get land value minus my documented sweat equity investment. |
| During construction | Neither of us can exit unilaterally. We must complete the project. If one of us doesn't perform, that party loses their share of the promote/upside. |
| After construction | Standard waterfall: capital returned first (you get yours back first, LIFO), then preferred returns (10%), then profit split (70% to you, 30% to me as promote). |

**Key point:** In every scenario, your capital is protected first. The waterfall ensures you get your money back before I see any promote.

---

### 2. "67/33 ownership but funding is 94/6 — where's the balance?"

This is the GP/LP structure. Your cash investment is 250K (94% of Round 1). My cash is 15K (6%). But my 33% ownership reflects:

- **Sweat equity (~46,000):** All the work I've done and will do — sourcing the deal, managing the project, licensing, contractors, banking, architect coordination. This is valued at ~46K in the business model.
- **GP role:** I'm the developer. I manage the project full-time. In standard GP/LP deals, the GP gets 20-35% for management + execution. Our 33% is within market range.
- **Your protection:** Even though I own 33%, the waterfall means you get ALL your capital back + 10% preferred return BEFORE any profit is split. And on the profit split, you get 70% and I get 30%.

**Bottom line:** Your 250K investment gets a 1.45x multiple (21.6% annual IRR). My 33% ownership compensates for sweat equity + management risk, but it doesn't dilute your economic returns.

We'll document the sweat equity explicitly in the pacto parassocial so it's clear and transparent.

---

### 3. "Sole manager with single-signature — what safeguards?"

You're right to flag this. Here's what we're adding:

- **Dual-signature for bank transactions above 10,000.** Below that, I can operate day-to-day (pay contractors, handle admin). Above that, both of us sign.
- **Manager replacement rights:** Under Portuguese law, a 75% quota vote can replace the manager. Since you hold 67%, you can replace me if you find another 8% vote (or if we set it at 67% in the pacto). We'll define specific triggers: fraud, gross negligence, abandonment, material breach.
- **Key-person clause:** If I can't continue (health, force majeure), you have the right to appoint a replacement or trigger an orderly dissolution.

---

### 4. "Where are the points of no return?"

Defined by project milestones:

1. **Before signing (now):** Full exit possible for either party, no cost
2. **After signing / before land:** Exit costs are minimal (legal fees only)
3. **After land purchase:** Land is committed. Exit means selling the land. You retain the asset.
4. **After construction starts:** No exit. We complete or we sell the project as-is (waterfall applies to proceeds)
5. **After completion:** Standard waterfall on sales proceeds

Each milestone increases commitment. The pacto parassocial will define what happens at each point.

### 5. Governance / Decision-Making

| Decision size | Who approves | How fast |
|--------------|-------------|----------|
| Under 5,000 | Me (Asaf), reported to you monthly | Same day |
| 5,000 to 15,000 | I propose, you have 48 hours to refuse | 48 hours |
| Over 15,000 or >5% budget change | Both of us must agree in writing | 5 business days |
| Major decisions (new debt, dissolution, selling land) | Both of us must agree (unanimous) | As needed |

Plus monthly financial reports from Roy, and a deadlock mechanism (independent mediator) if we can't agree.
