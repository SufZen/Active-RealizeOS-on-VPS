## Purpose

This document outlines the terms to be included in a Pacto Parassocial (shareholders' agreement) between the two shareholders of Escorpiao LDA. The pacto parassocial supplements the articles of association and governs the investment economics, governance, and exit mechanics of the Arena residential development project.

## 3. Capital Structure

### 3.1 Equity (Round 1)
| Investor | Amount | % of Round | Preferred Return |

### 3.2 Developer Sweat Equity
- Realization (GP) contributes sweat equity valued at ~46,000
- This represents project management, deal sourcing, licensing, contractor management, banking relationships, and strategic oversight
- Sweat equity is recognized as an economic claim on the promote, not as additional quota ownership
- The 33% quota ownership reflects both cash (15,000) and sweat equity value

### 3.3 Future Capital Rounds (Mezzanine Debt)
- Rounds 2-4 are structured as **mezzanine debt**, not equity
- These do not dilute quota ownership
- Terms per round:

| Round | Amount | Type | Interest Rate |
|-------|--------|------|---------------|
| Round 2 | 380,000 | Mezzanine debt | 20% |
| Round 3 | 436,000 | Mezzanine debt | 18% |
| Round 4 | 296,000 | Mezzanine debt | 16% |

### 3.4 Bank Financing
- Lender: NovoBanco
- Amount: ~770,000 (60% LTC)
- Interest: 5%
- Duration: 15 months

## 4. Capital Waterfall (Distribution Priority)

At project exit (sale of units), proceeds are distributed in this order:

### Step 1 — Return of Capital (LIFO)
1. Repay bank loan (NovoBanco)
2. Return Round 4 capital + interest (16%)
3. Return Round 3 capital + interest (18%)
4. Return Round 2 capital + interest (20%)
5. Return Round 1 capital (265,000 principal)
6. Return Developer sweat equity (46,000)

### Step 2 — Preferred Returns
7. Pay Round 1 preferred return (10% annual on 265,000)

### Step 3 — Promote / Upside Split
8. Remaining profit split:
   - **30% to GP (Realization)** — Developer Promote
   - **70% to LP (Zodiaco Badalado)** — LP Upside, distributed pro-rata to Round 1 investors

## 5. Governance — Tiered Decision Matrix

### 5.1 Decision Thresholds

| Threshold | Approval Required | Response Time |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| Operational (<5,000) | GP (Asaf) unilateral | Same day, reported monthly |
| Significant (5,000-15,000) | GP proposes, LP has right of refusal | 48 hours |
| Strategic (>15,000 or >5% budget variance) | Joint written approval (GP + LP) | 5 business days |
| Fundamental | Unanimous consent | As needed |

### 5.2 Fundamental Decisions (always unanimous)
- Dissolution or winding up of the SPV
- New equity issuance or dilution
- New debt beyond approved capital stack
- Sale of land or project assets (outside normal unit sales)
- Change of project scope (units, location, use)
- Admission of new shareholders
- Related-party transactions

### 5.3 Banking Controls
- **Dual-signature required** for all bank transactions above 10,000
- Single-signature (GP) permitted for transactions below 10,000
- Monthly bank statement provided to LP with financial report

## 6. Manager Rights and Obligations

### 6.1 Current Structure
- Asaf Eyzenkot (via Realization) serves as sole manager (gerente)

### 6.2 Manager Replacement
- Manager replacement requires 75% quota vote (per Article 257 CSC)
- LP (67%) can call a replacement vote upon occurrence of defined triggers

### 6.3 Replacement Triggers (Cause)
- Fraud or misappropriation of company funds
- Gross negligence in project management
- Material breach of pacto parassocial terms
- Abandonment of project responsibilities (>30 consecutive days without justified cause)
- Criminal conviction related to business activities
- Failure to provide required financial reports for 2+ consecutive months

### 6.4 Key-Person Clause
- If Asaf Eyzenkot is unable to continue as manager (death, incapacity, force majeure):
  - LP has the right to appoint a replacement manager within 60 days
  - If no replacement is appointed, either party may trigger orderly dissolution
  - GP's promote rights are preserved for work completed up to the event date

---

## 7. Dissolution and Exit by Project Phase

### Phase 1: Pre-Land Acquisition
- Either party may exit by written notice
- Capital returned pro-rata within 30 days
- Costs incurred to date shared proportionally

### Phase 2: Post-Land, Pre-Construction
- **If GP exits:** LP retains full ownership of land and SPV. GP forfeits promote.
- **If LP exits:** LP receives fair market value of land (independent valuation) minus GP's documented sweat equity investment to date. GP retains the SPV.
- Land valuation by independent certified evaluator (perito avaliador)

### Phase 3: During Construction
- **No unilateral exit permitted**
- Forced completion of project
- Non-performing party:
  - Loses promote/upside entitlement
  - Remains liable for committed capital
  - Other party may assume operational control
- If both parties agree to stop: orderly wind-down, assets sold, waterfall applies to proceeds

### Phase 4: Post-Construction / Sales Phase
- Standard waterfall applies to all sales proceeds
- Final distribution within 90 days of last unit sale
- Remaining assets (if any) distributed per waterfall

## 9. Capital Call Procedures

- Capital calls for Rounds 2-4 require:
  - 30 days written notice to investors
  - Purpose and budget justification
  - Current project status report
- Failure to meet a capital call:
  - 15-day cure period
  - If uncured: non-contributing party's promote/upside is diluted proportionally

---

## 10. Deadlock Resolution

1. **Good faith negotiation:** 15 days
2. **Independent mediator:** Appointed within 10 days if negotiation fails. Costs shared equally.
3. **If mediation fails:** Dispute submitted to Portuguese courts (Comarca de Setubal)

---

## 11. Non-Compete and Confidentiality

- Both parties agree not to develop competing residential projects in Barreiro municipality during the project term + 12 months
- All project financial information, investor data, and commercial terms are confidential
- Confidentiality survives termination for 3 years

---

## 12. Tag-Along / Drag-Along

### Tag-Along
- If either party receives a bona fide offer to purchase their quota:
  - Other party has the right to sell their quota on the same terms
  - 30-day notice period

### Drag-Along
- If a party holding 67%+ of quotas receives a bona fide offer for the entire SPV:
  - May require the minority to sell on the same terms
  - Minimum price must satisfy waterfall (all capital + preferred returns covered)

---

## 13. Amendments

- This pacto parassocial may only be amended by written agreement signed by both parties
- No oral modifications
