# MioLiving Partnership — Financial Model

## Initial Capital

Each of the 4 founding partners contributes **€800**, totaling **€3,200** in initial partnership capital.

Funds are managed in Shirly's company account (designated partnership account).

**Priority allocation:**
1. Branding — presentations, landing page, visual identity
2. Tech tools — CRM platform and automation infrastructure

---

## The 20/80 Split

Every revenue event in the partnership is split at the top level into two pools:

| Pool | Share | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Partnership Fund | 20% | Collective growth, operational reserve, shared expenses |
| Performance Matrix | 80% | Distributed to partners based on role in each deal |

---

## Partnership Fund Rules (the 20%)

**Operational floor:** A minimum of **€1,500** must always remain in the fund. This floor is never distributed — it ensures operational continuity.

**Quarterly dividend:** Any amount above the €1,500 floor is distributed to partners on the **20th of January, April, July, and October** each year.

The fund covers shared operating costs (tools, legal, accounting, marketing) before distribution. Surplus above the floor and shared costs is the distributable dividend.

---

## Performance Matrix (the 80%)

The 80% performance pool is distributed differently depending on which business arm generated the revenue. Each arm has three contribution categories:

- **Sourcing** — finding and originating the deal or client
- **Sales / Capital** — converting, closing, or bringing investor capital
- **Execution / Management** — delivering the service, managing the ground operation

| Business Arm | Sourcing | Sales / Capital | Execution / Management | Logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Retail Investor Escort | 30% | 30% | 40% | Ongoing execution (bureaucracy, ground coordination) requires the most resources |
| Residential Development (Flip) | 20% | 20% | 60% | Value center is renovation, planning, and project management |
| Hospitality — Deal Making | 40% | 40% | 20% | Main value is identifying the deal and connecting to a large investor |
| Venture Building | 10% | 10% | 80% | Pure professional advisory — heavy on strategy and systems hours |
| Design & Interior Design | 10% | 10% | 80% | Pure professional work (design and planning output) |

### Reading the Matrix

The percentages in each row sum to 100% of the 80% performance pool for that deal. The partner(s) who performed each function in a specific transaction receive the corresponding share.

**Example — Retail Investor Escort deal worth €10,000 total fee:**
- Partnership Fund (20%): €2,000
- Performance pool (80%): €8,000
  - Sourcing partner(s): €2,400 (30% of €8,000)
  - Sales partner(s): €2,400 (30% of €8,000)
  - Execution partner(s): €3,200 (40% of €8,000)

In most deals, one partner may cover multiple roles. The matrix determines proportional allocation where multiple partners share a role.

## Financial Transparency

All financial actions — incoming revenue, outgoing expenses, fund balance, and deal statuses — are managed in the shared financial system maintained by Asaf. Every partner has visibility into the full financial picture at all times.
