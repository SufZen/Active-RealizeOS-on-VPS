# Conservatória do Registo Predial

## What This Is

The Conservatória do Registo Predial is Portugal's property registry office, operated under the IRN (Instituto dos Registos e do Notariado). It maintains the official legal record of every property in Portugal: who owns it, what mortgages are registered against it, any easements, restrictions, or pending processes. The registry is what makes ownership legally binding and visible to third parties.

Two documents flow through here constantly in property work:
- **Caderneta Predial** — the AT's (tax authority) factual description of the property (area, location, fiscal value). Technically issued by Finanças, but closely linked to registry records.
- **Certidão de Teor** — the Conservatória's document showing current ownership, registered charges, and description. This is the document needed for every property transaction.

## When You Need This

- Buying or selling any property (you need a current certidão de teor)
- Registering a new mortgage or cancelling an old one
- Updating ownership records after a property purchase (registo de aquisição)
- Checking if a property has any liens (ónus) or encumbrances before purchasing
- Updating the caderneta predial after renovation or building works
- Confirming that a property is free and clear before signing a promissory contract (CPCV)
- When a buyer's bank requests registry documentation for mortgage approval

## Required Documents

For requesting a certidão de teor:
- Property's article number (artigo matricial) — find this on the caderneta predial or your IMI bill
- Freguesia (parish) and municipality where the property is located
- Your NIF (for online requests)

For registering a property ownership change (after notarial deed):
- Notarial deed (escritura pública) or certified copy
- SISA / IMT payment receipt (proof you paid property transfer tax)
- IS (Imposto do Selo) payment receipt
- Caderneta predial for the property
- NIFs of both buyer and seller

For updating the caderneta predial (after works):
- Completed Modelo 1 IMI (submitted to AT, not Conservatória)
- Evidence of works: building permit, final inspection certificate (vistoria)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Obtaining a Certidão de Teor (Online — Preferred Method)

1. Go to predial.ine.pt (Registo Predial Online)
2. Create an account or login (NIF + password, or Chave Móvel Digital)
3. Select "Certidão de Teor"
4. Enter the property details:
   - Distrito, Concelho, Freguesia
   - Número de Artigo Matricial (from caderneta predial or IMI notice)
   - Alternatively, the Descrição Predial number if you have prior registry documents
5. Choose type:
   - "Certidão de Teor Simples" — current state of the registry (ownership + charges). Sufficient for most transactions.
   - "Certidão de Teor Permanente" — provides a link (código de acesso) that can be shared with banks/notaries and is continuously updated
6. Pay online (credit card / MB reference)
7. Download PDF immediately after payment

### Obtaining Caderneta Predial (Online)

The caderneta predial is technically a Finanças document, not a Conservatória one. Obtain it at:
1. portaldasfinancas.gov.pt > login > Consultar > Património > Imóveis
2. Select the property from your list
3. Download the caderneta predial urbana (urban) or rústica (rural)
4. Free and immediate

Alternatively: predial.ine.pt also links to caderneta information but AT portal is more direct.

### Registering a Property Ownership Change

This step happens after you have signed the escritura pública at the notary. The notary often handles this automatically (via the "Casa Pronta" service at Loja do Cidadão), but if registering independently:

1. Go to predial.ine.pt > "Apresentação de Pedido de Registo"
2. Or visit the Conservatória do Registo Predial in Setúbal in person
3. Submit:
   - Certidão notarial da escritura (certified copy of deed)
   - IMT payment document
   - IS payment document
4. The Conservatória issues an "Ap." (apresentação) number — this is your registration timestamp
5. Full registration typically confirmed within 5-10 business days
6. You will receive a confirmation and the updated certidão de teor

### Checking for Encumbrances Before Purchasing

Always obtain a fresh certidão de teor before signing any CPCV (promissory purchase/sale contract). Check for:
- "Hipoteca" — mortgage. Must be cancelled by the seller before or at the moment of deed signing
- "Penhora" — asset seizure by creditors. Blocks transfer until resolved
- "Usufruto" — usufruct rights. Previous owner may retain right to live in the property
- "Servidão" — easement (e.g., right of way). Affects use of land

If the property is registered in the name of a company (not an individual), also verify via the Registo Comercial that the company's legal representatives have authority to sell.

### Updating Caderneta After Renovation

1. Submit Modelo 1 IMI to AT (Finanças) — this triggers re-evaluation of the property's VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário)
2. AT will send a new caderneta predial reflecting updated VPT
3. The Conservatória's description may also need updating if physical dimensions changed (e.g., an extension added) — this requires a formal request (pedido de rectificação de descrição)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Not obtaining a fresh certidão de teor** before signing a CPCV — registry can change; always use one dated within the last few days of the transaction
- **Assuming the seller's mortgage is automatically discharged** — the bank releasing the mortgage must file a "cancelamento de hipoteca" with the Conservatória; confirm this happens on or before the deed date
- **Relying on the old owner's caderneta predial** — always download a fresh copy for your NIF after transfer is registered
- **Not registering in your name promptly after deed** — until the Conservatória shows you as owner, you have no legally enforceable ownership against third parties
- **Confusing artigo matricial (AT/tax number) with the Conservatória's descrição predial number** — they are different identifiers; you may need both
- **For HomeAid transactions**, ensure the company's representante legal signing the deed matches what is registered in the Registo Comercial (see irn-registo.md)
