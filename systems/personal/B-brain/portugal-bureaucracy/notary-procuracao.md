# Notary & Procuração (Power of Attorney)

## What This Is

A procuração is a legal Power of Attorney (POA) — a document by which you authorise another person (the procurador or mandatário) to act on your behalf in specific legal matters. In Portugal, notarial services are provided by licensed notaries (notários) who authenticate documents, certify signatures, officiate property purchase deeds (escrituras públicas), and issue certified copies.

When you are abroad — in Israel, Italy, or elsewhere — a procuração enables a trusted representative to sign property deeds, banking documents, or other legal instruments in your name without your physical presence. For a multi-property operator who travels internationally, this is one of the most operationally critical documents.

## When You Need This

- Signing a property purchase or sale deed (escritura) when you cannot be present in Portugal
- Authorising a lawyer or trusted person to deal with AT, Conservatória, or Câmara on your behalf
- Enabling someone to sign HomeAid documents when you are travelling
- Buying or selling property while abroad (very common situation — prepare before you travel)
- Obtaining Apostilled copies of documents for use in Israel or Italy
- Getting notarially certified copies of contracts, company documents, or deeds for international counterparties
- Renewing or terminating a procuração

## Required Documents

To grant a procuração at a Portuguese notary:
- Your Cartão de Cidadão (valid and current)
- Your NIF
- Identity documents of the procurador: their full legal name, NIF, address, and Cartão de Cidadão number or passport number
- Written or verbal description of the powers you want to grant (notary will draft the text)
- For property-specific procuração: full property address, artigo matricial, Conservatória description number, and intended transaction type

For company procuração (HomeAid transactions):
- Certidão de matrícula (company registration, issued within 3 months)
- NIPC
- Evidence of your authority as gerente (from certidão de matrícula)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Deciding: Procuração Geral vs Procuração Especial

**Procuração Geral (General):**
- Grants broad authority over many categories of acts
- Example: "I authorise X to manage all my real estate affairs in Portugal"
- Useful if you travel frequently and need ongoing flexibility for your procurador
- Some banks and counterparties request a more specific document for major transactions

**Procuração Especial (Special):**
- Grants authority for one specific act or defined category of acts
- Example: "I authorise X to sign the purchase deed for the property at [address], for a price not below €Y, and all related documents including mortgage discharge and registry submission"
- More commonly accepted without question by notaries, banks, and registries
- Recommended for each individual property transaction

Best practice: Keep a Procuração Geral on file with your lawyer for day-to-day administrative tasks (AT correspondence, Câmara queries, SS matters). Use a Procuração Especial for each specific property deal.

### What to Include in a Property Transaction Procuração

Your notary will draft the text, but ensure it explicitly covers:
- Full identification of grantor (you) and procurador (name, NIF, address)
- Specific property details: full address, artigo matricial, Conservatória descrição number
- Authority to sign: CPCV (promissory purchase/sale contract), escritura pública, mortgage documents, IMT payment, IS payment, property registry submission
- Price parameters: "for a price not below €X" or "not above €Y"
- Whether the procurador may sub-delegate to another person (usually: no)
- Validity period: 6-12 months is standard for a property deal; longer if the deal may be delayed
- Whether it covers both the deal and subsequent mortgage registration (if buyer is using financing)

### Finding and Booking a Notary in Setúbal

1. Search the official notary directory: ordemnotarios.pt > "Pesquisa de Notários" > filter by "Setúbal"
2. Multiple notaries operate in Setúbal city centre — any licensed notary can draft a procuração for any property in Portugal, regardless of where the property is located
3. Call the notary office: "Queria marcar para fazer uma procuração" — describe your purpose
4. They will tell you what to bring; some will send a draft for review beforehand
5. Appointment duration: 30-60 minutes for a procuração; 1.5-2 hours for a full escritura

### What Happens at the Notary Appointment

1. You arrive with all documents
2. The notary reads the procuração aloud
3. You confirm you understand and agree
4. You sign in the notary's presence — the notary authenticates your signature and confirms your identity
5. The notary keeps the original in their archive (livro notarial)
6. You receive certified copies (certidões) — one for you, one for your procurador
7. The procurador can now act within the defined scope until the procuração expires or you revoke it

### Granting a Procuração While Already Abroad

If you are already in Israel or Italy when a deal requires action:

**Option 1 — Portuguese Consulate:**
- Contact the Portuguese consulate in Israel (Tel Aviv) or Italy (Rome or Milan) in advance
- Book a consular appointment — consulates can execute a procuração following Portuguese law
- The consulate-issued document is fully valid in Portugal
- Lead time: 1-3 weeks (consulates are busy; book early)
- Bring: valid Cartão de Cidadão or Portuguese passport, procurador's details, description of powers

**Option 2 — Local Notary + Apostille:**
- Visit a local notary in Israel or Italy
- They draft a Power of Attorney following standard international practice
- Obtain an Apostille on the document:
  - Israel: Ministry of Justice (Misrad HaMishpat) — apostille service
  - Italy: Corte d'Appello or Prefettura of the issuing region
- Have the document translated into Portuguese by a certified translator (tradutor juramentado)
- Both Israel and Italy are Hague Convention signatories — Apostille is sufficient; no further legalisation needed
- Timeline: 5-15 days depending on consulate/apostille queue

**Option 3 — Plan ahead:**
The most practical option: sign the procuração before departing Portugal. If you know a deal is imminent, do this at a Setúbal notary before travelling.

### Notary Authentication vs Apostille

**Reconhecimento de Assinatura (Signature Authentication):**
- The notary certifies that a signature on an existing document is genuine
- Two types: simples (confirms signature) and com menções especiais (confirms signer had authority to sign in a stated capacity)
- Used when documents are prepared outside the notary but need authentication for legal use

**Apostille:**
- International certification confirming that a document issued in one country is genuine, for use in another Hague Convention country
- For Portuguese documents to be used in Israel or Italy: request Apostille from the competent Portuguese authority (varies by document type — IRN for notarial documents, AT for tax certificates, IRN for registry certificates)
- For Israeli or Italian documents to be used in Portugal: Apostille from the issuing country, then certified Portuguese translation
- Apostille requests in Portugal: irn.mj.pt > "Apostilha" or at the relevant IRN office

**Certidões (Notarial Certified Copies):**
- The notary can issue certified copies of documents held in their archive (escrituras, procurações they executed)
- Essential when the original deed is at the notary but you need a copy for a bank, registry, or foreign partner

### Property Sale / Purchase Deed (Escritura)

All parties (or their procuração holders) must attend at the notary on the deed date:

Before the appointment, ensure:
- IMT (Imposto Municipal sobre Transmissões Onerosas de Imóveis) paid via AT portal
- IS (Imposto do Selo) paid at same time as IMT
- All certidões are recent: certidão de teor (property registry) dated within 5 days, caderneta predial current
- Licença de utilização (habitation certificate) available — if missing for older properties, the notary can execute a justificação notarial, but resolve this before the day
- Energy certificate (Certificado de Desempenho Energético) from ADENE
- Payment method agreed with the other party: bank transfer (most common) or cheque visado

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Procuração too narrow** — if it doesn't explicitly cover signing the mortgage (hipoteca) or paying IMT, a bank or notary will refuse to proceed with the procurador
- **Procuração too broad** — a general POA with no property-specific limits creates legal risk; a counterparty might also be reluctant to rely on it
- **Not checking the procurador's availability for the deed date** — confirm their calendar before granting the procuração for a specific transaction
- **Using an expired procuração** — property deals regularly take longer than expected; include a 12-month validity or be ready to renew
- **Foreign document without Apostille** — a notarised document from Israel without Apostille has no legal standing in Portugal; the Apostille is the essential authentication step
- **Missing certified translation** — documents in Hebrew or Italian must be translated by a certified Portuguese translator before a notary or registry will accept them
- **Company procuração without authority evidence** — if HomeAid is buying/selling, the notary needs to confirm you, as gerente, have authority to sign; bring the certidão de matrícula and any company resolution authorising the deal
