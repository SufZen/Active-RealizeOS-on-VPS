# Segurança Social (Employer)

## What This Is

Segurança Social (SS) is Portugal's social security system, equivalent to national insurance. As the employer at HomeAid with 5 employees, you have mandatory monthly obligations: calculating, declaring, and paying social security contributions for each employee and for the company. Failure to meet these obligations results in significant penalties and enforcement action.

The main digital platform for employers is Segurança Social Direta (seg-social.pt).

## When You Need This

- Every month: submitting the DRI (Declaração de Remunerações Individuais) and paying contributions
- When hiring a new employee: registering them with SS before their first day
- When an employee leaves: filing the cessation declaration
- Checking individual employee records (for maternity leave, parental leave, illness benefit processing)
- Responding to SS inspection requests or resolving discrepancies
- Checking your company's SS compliance status (for certidão de situação contributiva)
- Understanding what contributions HomeAid pays vs what employees contribute

## Required Documents

- NIPC (HomeAid's company tax number)
- Employee NIFs and SS numbers (número de beneficiário)
- Employee salaries (monthly gross)
- Employee contract start/end dates
- Access to Segurança Social Direta (seg-social.pt) — employer login

## Step-by-Step Process

### Registering HomeAid as an Employer (if not yet done)

1. Go to seg-social.pt > login with company NIPC + password (or Chave Móvel Digital)
2. Navigate: Empregadores > Inscrição como Empregador
3. Or visit the Setúbal SS office in person with:
   - Certidão de matrícula (company registration)
   - NIPC
   - Identity documents of the gerente
4. Once registered, HomeAid is assigned an employer identification number (NIE — Número de Identificação de Entidade)
5. This registration is what links monthly salary declarations to HomeAid

### Registering a New Employee (Before Their First Day)

This is a critical legal requirement — registration must happen before or on the first day of work:

1. Login to Segurança Social Direta > Empregadores > Admissão de Trabalhadores
2. Fill: employee NIF, name, date of birth, SS number, contract type, start date, salary
3. Submit — employee immediately appears in HomeAid's worker registry
4. Alternatively: login and use "Declaração de Admissão" (Admission Declaration)
5. If the employee does not yet have a SS number (número de beneficiário): they must register individually at SS office; as employer you can help facilitate this

**Important:** SS registration is independent of the labour contract (contrato de trabalho). Even if the written contract is not yet signed, the SS admission must be filed on day 1.

### Monthly DRI Submission (Declaração de Remunerações)

This is the most recurring task — submit every month:

1. Login to seg-social.pt > Empregadores > Declaração de Remunerações
2. Select period (previous month)
3. The system may pre-fill employee names — verify and enter gross salary for each
4. Include: base salary + any regular allowances (meal allowance above exempt threshold, etc.)
5. **Deadline: 10th of the following month** (e.g., January DRI must be submitted by February 10)
6. Submit the declaration
7. A payment reference (referência de pagamento) is generated
8. Pay via: Multibanco, online banking MB reference, or direct debit
9. **Payment deadline: same date — 10th of the following month**

Your accountant should handle DRI submissions as part of the monthly payroll process — ensure they have Procuração Eletrónica for Segurança Social.

### Contribution Rates

For the standard general regime (regime geral):

| Contribution Type | Rate | Who Pays |
|---|---|---|
| Employer (Taxa Social Única — TSU employer) | 23.75% | HomeAid pays on top of salary |
| Employee | 11.00% | Deducted from employee gross salary |
| Total SS cost to HomeAid per employee | 34.75% of gross salary | Combined |

Example: Employee earns €1,000 gross/month
- HomeAid pays SS: €237.50
- Employee SS deduction: €110.00 (withheld from salary, net salary before IRS withholding is €890)
- HomeAid's total employment cost: €1,237.50 + any other benefits

Note: Different rates apply for:
- Employees over 65 (reduced rates)
- First employment (temporary reductions available — check AT/SS for current incentives)
- Part-time employees (proportional to hours)
- Domestic workers (taxa reduzida)

### Processing Employee Termination (Declaração de Cessação)

When an employee leaves (resignation, dismissal, or end of fixed-term contract):

1. Login to seg-social.pt > Empregadores > Cessação de Trabalhadores
2. Fill: employee NIF, end date, reason for cessation (motivo: mutual agreement, resignation, disciplinary dismissal, etc.)
3. Submit before or on the last day of employment
4. Issue the final salary and any severance pay (compensação) — consult labour lawyer for dismissal amounts
5. The employee will need this record for unemployment benefit (subsídio de desemprego) if applicable
6. Keep the signed termination agreement (acordo de cessação) or dismissal letter on file

### Accessing Segurança Social Direta

1. seg-social.pt > "Acesso Directo"
2. Login with: NIPC + password, Chave Móvel Digital, or Cartão de Cidadão
3. Key sections for employers:
   - "Declarações de Remunerações" — monthly submissions
   - "Trabalhadores" — employee registry, admissions, cessations
   - "Contribuições" — payment history and outstanding amounts
   - "Certidões" — request compliance certificates
   - "Mensagens" — SS communications and notifications

### Getting a SS Compliance Certificate (Certidão de Situação Contributiva)

Required for: company contracts, public tenders, property transactions:
1. seg-social.pt > Certidões > Situação Contributiva
2. Select entity: HomeAid NIPC
3. If no outstanding contributions, certificate generated immediately
4. Valid for 90 days
5. If there are arrears: the certificate will not be issued until debts are cleared or a payment plan (plano prestacional) is in place

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Missing the 10th of the month deadline** — penalty is 30-60% of outstanding contributions, plus interest; repeat offences escalate rapidly
- **Not registering an employee on day 1** — this is one of the most common labour infractions. ACT (Autoridade para as Condições do Trabalho) conducts inspections; unregistered employees on the date of inspection mean immediate fines (€1,000-5,000+ per employee)
- **Declaring incorrect salaries** — the DRI must match the payslips and the IRS withholding declarations; discrepancies trigger audits
- **Forgetting meal allowance rules** — the exempt portion (€6 per day for cash; €8.32 for card/voucher as of recent values) is not subject to SS; amounts above these are SS-liable. Confirm current exempt values with your accountant each year
- **Not separating employer SS from employee SS** — employer pays 23.75% on top; employee's 11% is withheld from their gross salary. Both amounts go to SS but come from different sources
- **Not filing cessation declaration when employee leaves** — the employee cannot claim unemployment benefit; you remain technically liable for contributions
