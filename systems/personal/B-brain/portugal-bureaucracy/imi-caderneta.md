# IMI & Caderneta Predial

## What This Is

IMI (Imposto Municipal sobre Imóveis) is Portugal's annual municipal property tax — the Portuguese equivalent of council tax or rates, but calculated as a fixed percentage of the property's assessed fiscal value. Every property owner in Portugal pays IMI annually.

The VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário) is the official fiscal value assigned to each property by AT. This is not the same as market value — it is a formula-based assessment. IMI is calculated as a percentage of VPT.

The caderneta predial is the property's official fiscal identity card: it shows the VPT, permitted use, area, ownership, and matrix reference. It is a document you will need constantly — for sales, permits, mortgages, and tax filings.

As a multi-property owner in Setúbal, you receive separate IMI bills for each property (or a consolidated notice) and must track payment across all of them.

## When You Need This

- Every year: paying IMI bills on the correct schedule (April, July, November depending on amount)
- Before any property sale: confirm current VPT (it affects IMT — the transfer tax the buyer pays)
- After a renovation or extension: update the caderneta and potentially challenge the new VPT if over-assessed
- When buying a property: check the caderneta to understand ongoing IMI costs
- For mortgage applications or refinancing: banks request current cadernetas
- For IRS rental income declarations (Anexo F): property's artigo matricial is required
- When selling: buyer's lawyer will request the caderneta as part of due diligence

## Required Documents

- NIF (or NIPC for company-owned properties)
- AT Portal login credentials
- Property's artigo matricial (matrix reference number) — on any existing caderneta or IMI bill
- For VPT challenge (reclamação): survey evidence, comparable property assessments, photos
- For caderneta update after renovation: building permit (licença de obras), final inspection certificate (vistoria), as-built drawings

## Step-by-Step Process

### Understanding the IMI Payment Schedule

IMI is automatically calculated by AT each year — you do not submit anything. AT sends a bill (nota de cobrança) to your AT Portal inbox and, sometimes, by post.

Payment schedule based on total IMI owed per property:
- **IMI up to €100**: single payment in April
- **IMI between €100 and €500**: single payment in April
- **IMI between €500 and €1,000**: two payments — April (50%) and November (50%)
- **IMI above €1,000**: three payments — April (1/3), July (1/3), November (1/3)

Note: These thresholds apply per property, not across all your properties combined.

If you own multiple properties, each generates its own IMI bill. Create a calendar with all payment dates in March/April once AT publishes the bills.

### Paying IMI

1. Login to AT Portal (portaldasfinancas.gov.pt)
2. Navigate: Serviços > Consultar > Notas de Cobrança de IMI
3. Each property has its own payment reference
4. Pay via:
   - AT Portal > generate Multibanco reference > pay at any ATM or online banking
   - Directly at an MB ATM: "Pagamentos e Transferências" > "Serviços" > AT reference
   - Direct debit: set up at AT Portal for automatic payment
5. For company-owned properties (HomeAid): login with NIPC, same process

### Getting a Caderneta Predial

The caderneta is free and immediate:

1. Login to portaldasfinancas.gov.pt
2. Navigate: Património > Predial > Imóveis > select the property
3. Click "Caderneta Predial" and download the PDF
4. The caderneta shows:
   - Artigo matricial (matrix reference number)
   - Property address and description
   - Permitted use (habitação, comércio, serviços, etc.)
   - Gross area and dependent areas
   - Current VPT
   - Owner(s) and their NIFs

For older transactions or foreign counterparties unfamiliar with the online system: bring your NIF and the artigo matricial to the local Serviço de Finanças in Setúbal and request a printed certified copy.

After buying a property: the caderneta updates automatically within 30-60 days as AT processes the escritura and Conservatória registration. No action needed — check back in 60 days.

### How IMI Is Calculated

IMI = VPT x municipal tax rate (taxa municipal)

The Câmara Municipal de Setúbal sets the rate annually. For urban buildings (prédios urbanos):
- Typical range: 0.30% to 0.45% of VPT
- Setúbal's rate: check the current year's published rate at the AT portal or Câmara's website
- Rural land (rústico): 0.80% (fixed nationally)

Example: VPT €150,000 at 0.35% rate = €525 IMI/year (two payments: €262.50 in April and €262.50 in November)

The VPT formula considers: area, age, condition, location coefficient, quality and comfort coefficient, and permitted use. VPT is generally 30-60% of market value in Setúbal.

### IMI Exemptions and Reductions

**Primary Residence Exemption (Isenção para Habitação Própria e Permanente):**
- Properties where you are registered as having your habitual residence may qualify for an IMI reduction
- Setúbal Câmara may apply a municipal discount (majoração/desconto) — check annually
- Families with dependents receive additional reductions (Código do IRS-based)

**Rehabilitation Exemption:**
- Properties that have been rehabilitated in eligible areas (zonas de reabilitação urbana) can be exempt from IMI for 3 years
- Apply at the local Serviço de Finanças after works completion; requires Câmara certification of rehabilitation
- Worth investigating for renovation projects in Setúbal's historic centre

**Affordable Housing:**
- Properties rented out under the Programa de Arrendamento Acessível (affordable rent programme) qualify for IMI exemption — relevant if any of your properties participate

### Challenging an IMI Assessment / Disputing VPT

If you believe the VPT is too high (and therefore your IMI is excessive):

1. **Timeline is strict**: you must act within 30 days of receiving the updated caderneta showing the new VPT
2. Submit a Reclamação de VPT via AT Portal > Predial > Reclamação de Avaliação
3. Or in person at the local Serviço de Finanças in Setúbal
4. Include supporting evidence:
   - Comparable VPTs for similar nearby properties (pull from AT Portal — search by artigo in the same rua or bairro)
   - Independent valuation report (if significant amounts)
   - Photos showing actual condition vs assumed condition
5. AT conducts a new inspection and may revise the VPT
6. If the reclamação fails: escalate via impugnação judicial (administrative court) within 90 days — engage a tax lawyer for this step; worth it only for large assessed overvalues

### Updating Caderneta After Renovation (Modelo 1 IMI)

If you have added area, changed permitted use, or substantially renovated:

1. Submit a Declaração Modelo 1 IMI at the local Serviço de Finanças:
   - Online: AT Portal > Predial > Declaração Modelo 1 (if your Finanças office supports it)
   - In person: Serviço de Finanças de Setúbal with printed form
2. Attach: approved renovation plans, Câmara final inspection certificate (vistoria), habitation licence
3. AT will request an inspector visit for significant renovations
4. Timeline for reassessment: 3-6 months
5. Important: this reassessment will likely increase the VPT if you have added value — factor this into your renovation economics. The new VPT also determines the IMT if you sell, so a higher VPT benefits the buyer's IMT calculation.

### Managing IMI Across Multiple Properties

Practical steps:
1. Maintain a property register (spreadsheet): list each property, its artigo matricial, VPT, annual IMI, payment dates
2. Download all cadernetas at the start of each year to verify nothing has changed
3. Set calendar reminders: April 1 (check bills), April 30 (first payment deadline), July 31 (second payment if applicable), November 30 (third payment)
4. For HomeAid-owned properties: ensure IMI bills go to the company NIPC login on AT Portal, not your personal NIF
5. If you've recently sold a property: confirm with AT that it is no longer in your name for IMI purposes; delays in registry updates can cause erroneous IMI bills

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Missing the April payment** — IMI late payment penalties start immediately and interest accrues; AT can move to enforcement (execução fiscal) after a brief grace period
- **Not verifying all properties appear on the IMI bill** — after a purchase, verify the property transfers to your NIF bill by the following year; if it doesn't, follow up with the local Finanças
- **Ignoring VPT updates** — when AT conducts a general area reassessment (avaliação geral), VPTs often change significantly; set a 30-day challenge window reminder whenever you receive an updated caderneta
- **Confusing VPT with market value** — VPT is lower than market value; do not use it as the basis for pricing a property for sale
- **Not filing Modelo 1 after significant renovation** — failing to declare added area is a tax avoidance risk; AT can back-assess with penalties
- **Mixing personal and company IMI** — if a property moves from your name to HomeAid's (or vice versa), ensure AT records match; both your personal NIF and the NIPC must be checked each year
