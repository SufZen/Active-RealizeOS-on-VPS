# IRN — Instituto dos Registos e do Notariado

## What This Is

IRN (Instituto dos Registos e do Notariado) manages Portugal's official registration services for both personal civil matters (Registo Civil — births, marriages, ID documents) and company commercial matters (Registo Comercial — company incorporation, changes, certidões de matrícula). As both a Portuguese citizen and a company director, you interact with IRN for life events and for HomeAid's and Realization's corporate obligations.

This guide covers two distinct sections:
- **PERSONAL**: Registo Civil — Cartão de Cidadão, birth/marriage certificates, Apostille
- **COMPANY**: Registo Comercial — certidão de matrícula, Código de Acesso, IES, RCBE, company publications

## When You Need This

### Personal (Registo Civil):
- Renewing your Cartão de Cidadão (Portuguese ID card)
- Requesting birth certificates, marriage certificates, or other civil records
- Registering a child's birth
- Apostille on Portuguese documents for use abroad (Israel, Italy)

### Company (Registo Comercial — HomeAid):
- Requesting a certidão de matrícula (company registration certificate) — required for bank accounts, contracts, property purchases, public tenders
- Registering changes: director appointments/resignations, address updates, share capital changes
- Filing annual accounts summary (acta de aprovação de contas) — required by June 30
- Requesting certidão permanente (online access to live company record)
- Dissolving or liquidating a company (if applicable)
- Publishing acts in eJustiça (Diário da República online)

## Required Documents

### Cartão de Cidadão Renewal:
- Previous Cartão de Cidadão
- NIF
- Payment: €15 standard (€25 urgente)

### Civil Certificates (certidões de nascimento, casamento):
- Your NIF
- Names and approximate dates of the act (birth date, marriage date)

### Certidão de Matrícula (Company):
- HomeAid NIPC
- (Requestable online without any other document)

### Company Changes (address, directors):
- Current certidão permanente
- Acta da assembleia geral (notarized minutes of the general meeting approving the change)
- Identification of new/removed directors (NIF + Cartão de Cidadão)
- If online submission: qualified electronic signature or CMD authentication

## Step-by-Step Process

### Renewing Cartão de Cidadão

1. Book online at agendamento.irn.mj.pt or go to your local IRN/Loja do Cidadão
2. In-person appointment at IRN Setúbal or Loja do Cidadão Setúbal
3. Bring: expired or expiring CC + NIF + payment card
4. Biometric data captured at counter (fingerprints, photo)
5. New card issued in 5-10 business days; sent by post or collected in person
6. The Cartão de Cidadão also serves as your NIF card, voter registration, and health card (SNS number embedded)
7. PIN activation: activate PIN at any Multibanco machine > "Ativar/Desbloquear CC"

### Requesting Civil Certificates (Certidões de Registo Civil)

1. Online at irn.mj.pt > Registos Civis > Pedir Certidão
2. Select type: nascimento (birth), casamento (marriage), óbito (death), etc.
3. Provide name, date, and Civil Registry office of the original registration
4. Pay online (€10-15)
5. Certificate sent by post (5-10 business days) or available for download if digital format accepted
6. For foreign use: may need Apostille (see below)

### Apostille on Portuguese Documents

For Portuguese documents to be used in Israel, Italy, or other countries:
1. Online: irn.mj.pt > Apostila da Convenção de Haia
2. Or in person at any IRN or Conservatória do Registo Civil
3. The Apostille certifies the signature/seal of the Portuguese authority who issued the document
4. Cost: €15-20 per document
5. Processing: 1-5 business days
6. Used for: birth certificates for Israeli authorities, company documents for Italian deals, powers of attorney

### Certidão de Matrícula / Certidão Permanente (Company)

**Certidão de Matrícula** (point-in-time snapshot):
1. Online: portaldocidadao.pt > Serviços Empresariais > Certidões
2. Or: publicações.mj.pt
3. Search by HomeAid NIPC
4. Instant download, €2-5
5. Valid for most purposes for 3-12 months depending on requester

**Certidão Permanente** (live online access):
1. Go to eportugal.gov.pt or rcbe.mj.pt
2. Activate access for HomeAid NIPC — €25/year for a permanent access code
3. Share the code with banks, partners, or public entities — they access the live record directly
4. Eliminates the need to repeatedly issue certidão de matrícula printouts
5. Recommended: set up once, renew annually

### Registering Company Changes (HomeAid)

When HomeAid has a change in directors, address, share capital, or articles of association:

1. Prepare: acta notarial (notarized minutes of general meeting) approving the change
2. Submit online at rcbe.mj.pt (Registo Comercial Bilingue e Electrónico) using CMD authentication
3. Or: in person at Conservatória do Registo Comercial (Setúbal or Lisbon)
4. Fees: €75-200 depending on act type
5. Registration confirmed in 1-5 business days
6. After registration: publish in eJustiça (Diário da República online) — required for some acts (director changes, capital increases)

### Publishing Acts in eJustiça / Diário da República

Some company acts must be published in the official gazette:
1. Go to: publicacoes.mj.pt or ejusticia.mj.pt
2. Log in with CMD or NIF/password
3. Select: "Publicações Obrigatórias das Sociedades"
4. Upload the act (signed minutes or deed extract)
5. Cost: €30-75 per publication
6. Publication appears within 1-3 business days
7. Required for: certain director appointments, capital changes, merger/dissolution notices

### Annual Accounts Filing (IES — Informação Empresarial Simplificada)

The IES is Portugal's combined annual company filing. It simultaneously submits financial data to AT (for IRC purposes), INE (statistics), and the Registo Comercial (for transparency):

1. Filed via AT Portal: portaldasfinancas.gov.pt > IRC > IES/DA
2. **Deadline: 15 July** of the year following the fiscal year (e.g., 2025 accounts due by 15 July 2026)
3. Prepared exclusively by your certified accountant (contabilista certificado) — mandatory by law
4. Includes: balance sheet (balanço), income statement (demonstração de resultados), list of employees and remunerations, notes to accounts
5. Late submission penalty: €200 minimum, escalating with delay
6. This filing also updates the Registo Comercial with the annual accounts — the company's public record remains current

The shareholders' general meeting (assembleia geral) to approve accounts must occur before the filing deadline. Document this in an ata (minutes) and keep it in the company's livro de atas.

### Beneficial Ownership Registry (RCBE — Registo Central do Beneficiário Efetivo)

All Portuguese companies must register the individuals who ultimately own or control them:

1. Register at: rcbe.justica.gov.pt
2. Login with Cartão de Cidadão / CMD as gerente of HomeAid or Realization
3. Declare: all individuals owning or controlling 25%+ of the company (shares, voting rights, or other means)
4. First registration: within 30 days of company incorporation
5. Annual update: confirm or update the declaration once per year — tie this to IES filing in July
6. Penalty for non-compliance: €1,000 for initial failure; recurring fines thereafter
7. Banks now routinely require RCBE compliance as part of KYC — they will check before opening accounts or during periodic reviews

### Código de Acesso (Online Verification Code)

The Certidão Permanente includes a Código de Acesso — a unique code that allows anyone to verify the company's live registry status online without you issuing a new document:

1. Share this code with: banks, foreign partners, notaries, public procurement processes
2. Recipients verify at: irn.mj.pt or publicacoes.mj.pt using the code
3. They see current gerentes, capital, address, and status — in real time
4. This eliminates the need to provide a fresh certidão de matrícula for every request
5. Store the code in your documents system and your accountant's records

### Annual Filing Requirement (Acta de Aprovação de Contas)

HomeAid must approve annual accounts every year:
1. Shareholders meeting approves accounts (until June 30 of the following year — practically aligned with IES deadline)
2. Draft acta (minutes) recording approval of: balanço (balance sheet), demonstração de resultados (income statement), results appropriation
3. File with Registo Comercial via rcbe.mj.pt by June 30 (or aligned with IES filing in July)
4. IES submission at AT Portal also covers Registo Comercial requirements — your accountant files both together
5. Failure to file: civil fine + company may be struck off (cancelamento oficioso) after 3 consecutive years of non-filing

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Letting Cartão de Cidadão expire** — you need it for notarial acts, bank openings, and many official processes; renew 60-90 days before expiry; an expired CC blocks CMD authentication as well
- **Requesting certidão de matrícula instead of certidão permanente** — the certidão permanente with its Código de Acesso is more practical for ongoing business; set it up once and share the code rather than issuing new documents repeatedly
- **Not registering RCBE** — the RCBE annual declaration is easy to overlook; add it to your accountant's July checklist alongside IES filing; banks now enforce this during KYC reviews
- **Missing the IES/annual accounts filing deadline (15 July)** — the fine starts at €200 and escalates; consistent non-filing leads to compulsory striking off (cancelamento oficioso) after 3 years
- **Not activating CMD** — Chave Móvel Digital (CMD) is the single most useful authentication tool for all Portuguese government portals; if not yet activated, do it at any Loja do Cidadão with your CC and Portuguese mobile number
- **Apostille vs. translation confusion** — an Apostille certifies the signature on a document, not its content; for the content to be understood by foreign counterparties, a certified translation (tradutor juramentado) is also needed; clarify with the receiving party which they require
- **Publishing in wrong gazette section** — eJustiça has specific sections for different act types; confirm with your accountant which section applies before submitting
- **Not updating company address at Registo Comercial** — if the company's registered address changes, update the registry promptly; an outdated address means official correspondence is misdirected and banks may flag the discrepancy
