# Utilities & Bills (Multi-Property)

## What This Is

Managing utilities across multiple properties in Setúbal involves dealing with electricity (EDP/E.Redes), natural gas (EDP Gás/Galp), water (SMAS Setúbal), and internet/telecoms. Each property requires its own contracts, and when buying, selling, or renting, these contracts must be correctly transferred or set up. For HomeAid-managed properties, there are additional practical considerations for centralising bill management.

## When You Need This

- Setting up utilities for a newly acquired or renovated property
- Transferring EDP electricity contract to a new buyer after a sale
- Moving water/gas contracts from one name to another
- Managing multiple EDP contracts under a single account (for efficiency)
- Setting up internet at a rental property
- Organising bill payments across HomeAid-managed properties
- Understanding billing cycles and how to handle unpaid bills from previous tenants

## Required Documents

### For EDP (Electricity) Contracts
- NIF of the account holder (personal or company NIPC)
- Cartão de Cidadão or passport
- CPE (Código do Ponto de Entrega) — the unique electricity supply point identifier; found on any existing EDP bill or on the meter
- IBAN (for direct debit / débito directo)
- For company contracts: certidão de matrícula + gerente's NIF

### For Gas (Galp / EDP Gás)
- NIF
- CUI (Código Universal de Instalação de Gás) — the gas supply point identifier; on any existing gas bill
- Proof of valid gas installation (certificação da instalação de gás)

### For Water (SMAS Setúbal)
- NIF
- Cartão de Cidadão
- Caderneta predial (confirming ownership) or rental contract (for tenant setup)
- Property address and SMAS account number (conta de cliente) if taking over an existing contract

## Step-by-Step Process

### Managing Multiple EDP Electricity Contracts

1. Go to edp.pt > login (or create an account with your NIF)
2. All your CPEs can be linked to one online account — this gives a single dashboard view of all properties
3. Under "Gerir Contratos" you can view consumption, bills, payment status for each property
4. Set up direct debit (débito directo) on each contract — this avoids late fees and manual payments
5. For HomeAid-managed rental properties: decide whether contracts stay in owner's name (simpler for management) or tenant's name (cleaner legally). For short-term rentals, keep in owner's name.

### Transferring EDP Contract to a New Owner After Property Sale

Do this on the day of, or immediately after, the notarial deed (escritura):

1. The new owner contacts EDP to set up a new contract: edp.pt/particulares > "Mudar de Comercializador/Titular"
   - OR call EDP: **808 504 504**
2. Alternatively, EDP has an automatic transfer service — the notary or real estate agent can initiate this at the time of deed signing
3. Information needed: CPE, new owner's NIF and IBAN, date of transfer
4. EDP takes a meter reading on the transfer date — final bill goes to old owner, new contract starts for new owner
5. You remain responsible for any outstanding bills on the old contract — clear these before the sale closes
6. If the property has been vacant, EDP may have sent the contract into "fornecimento suspenso" — the new owner will need to request reconnection

### Setting Up EDP for a New Rental Property

1. Confirm CPE of the property (check meter box or call EDP)
2. If the property has never had an active EDP contract (brand new build): contact EDP to activate the supply point — they will need an electrical installation certificate (certificado de instalação elétrica from the DGEG portal)
3. Call EDP (808 504 504) or set up online at edp.pt
4. Choose tariff: for residential, select "Tarifa Simples" or "Bi-Horária" (bi-hourly, off-peak cheaper); for higher consumption, consider Social Tariff if the tenant qualifies
5. EDP Comercial vs EDP Distribuição: you are signing with a comercializador (commercial supplier); EDP Serviço Universal or a competitor. The grid (distribuição) is managed by E.Redes (separate entity).
6. If switching supplier: use Portal Misto (portalmisto.edp.pt) to see all available suppliers

### Gas (Galp / EDP Gás) in Setúbal

Setúbal has natural gas network coverage in many urban areas:
1. Check if your property has a gas supply point (contact Galp: galp.pt or **808 44 44 44**, or EDP Gás: edpgaz.pt)
2. Obtain the CUI from the existing bill or by calling the current supplier
3. For a new contract: galp.pt > "Energia" > "Gás Natural" > "Mudar para Galp"
4. Mandatory: valid gas installation certificate (certificação instalação gás) — must be renewed periodically by a certified technician (técnico certificado pela DGEG)
5. If a property has no piped gas: portable LPG (botijão de gás) is available at any petrol station or supermarket — no contract needed

### Water — SMAS Setúbal

The Serviços Municipalizados de Água e Saneamento (SMAS) de Setúbal manages water supply in the municipality:
1. For new contracts or ownership transfers: visit or call SMAS Setúbal (see contacts below)
2. Required: NIF, Cartão de Cidadão, proof of ownership (caderneta predial) or rental contract
3. Water bills arrive monthly or bimonthly — set up direct debit at SMAS office or via MBWAY/ATM using the reference on each bill
4. For multi-property management: SMAS may allow consolidated account management — ask at the office
5. If a property has been vacant: water may be shut off; request reconnection (reativação) with proof of ownership

### Internet Providers in Setúbal

Main options:
- **MEO** (meo.pt | 16 200): strong coverage in Setúbal; fibre available in most urban areas; bundles include TV
- **NOS** (nos.pt | 16 190): good alternative; fibre-based; competitive for bundles
- **Vodafone** (vodafone.pt | 16 910): mobile-first but has home fibre in Setúbal
- **Nowo** (nowo.pt): budget option; check coverage at your specific address

For rental properties:
- Consider whether to include internet in the rental price (simpler for tenants) or have them set up their own — for short-term/AL rentals, include it (it's expected)
- Most providers allow contract in owner's name with tenant using the connection
- Contract minimum: 24 months for most offers; early cancellation fees apply

### Bill Management for HomeAid-Managed Properties

Practical system:
1. Create a spreadsheet (or use a property management tool) with one row per property, columns for: EDP contract, gas contract, SMAS account, internet provider, bill due dates, payment method
2. Set all contracts to direct debit (débito directo) from the relevant bank account — eliminates manual payment risk
3. Receive paperless bills (fatura electrónica) — all providers offer this; reduces paper and makes digital archiving easy
4. File bills by property and year — needed for rental income expense declarations (Anexo F, IRS)
5. For HomeAid expenses: ensure bills for properties HomeAid manages are in a name that allows company expense deduction — discuss with accountant

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Not transferring EDP contract at the moment of property sale** — if the old owner forgets, the new owner is on the old contract; this causes billing disputes and can affect the old owner's credit
- **Leaving properties with active contracts after a sale** — you continue being billed until you formally terminate or transfer
- **Missing the gas installation certificate renewal** — expired certificates can void insurance and block contract activation; check renewal dates for each property
- **Not setting up direct debit** — manual payments are a common point of failure; missed bills accumulate interest and can go to debt collection
- **Using personal NIF for HomeAid property expenses** — if HomeAid is managing and billing clients for the properties, the utility contracts should match the entity being invoiced to allow expense deduction
- **Assuming internet is included in SMAS or EDP services** — each service is a separate contract with a separate provider
