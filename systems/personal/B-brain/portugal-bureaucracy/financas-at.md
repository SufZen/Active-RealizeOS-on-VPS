# Finanças / AT Portal

## What This Is

The Autoridade Tributária e Aduaneira (AT) is Portugal's tax authority. The AT Portal (portaldasfinancas.gov.pt) is the central platform for all personal and corporate tax obligations: filing returns, paying taxes, requesting certificates, and managing instalment plans. Every tax interaction in Portugal eventually passes through here.

## When You Need This

- Filing the annual IRS personal income tax return (Modelo 3) — due April 30
- Filing company IRC (corporate income tax) returns — due July 31
- Submitting quarterly IVA (VAT) declarations
- Requesting tax compliance certificates (certidão de não dívida)
- Paying tax bills or setting up payment plans
- Checking your tax situation across all entities (personal + HomeAid company)
- Issuing or receiving electronic invoices (faturas eletrónicas)

## Required Documents

- NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal) — your personal tax number
- NIPC (Número de Identificação de Pessoa Coletiva) — HomeAid's company tax number
- Password for the AT Portal (or Chave Móvel Digital / Cartão de Cidadão login)
- IBAN for any refunds
- All receipts, invoices, property income records for the tax year
- Employee payslips and SS declarations (for IRC)
- Rental contracts and income records for each property (Categoria F income)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Logging In

1. Go to portaldasfinancas.gov.pt
2. Click "Autenticar" (top right)
3. Login options:
   - NIF + password (simplest)
   - Chave Móvel Digital (CMD) — two-factor via phone
   - Cartão de Cidadão with card reader
4. To switch between personal (IRS) and company (IRC/IVA) views, use the entity selector after login — look for "Contribuinte" dropdown showing your NIF or NIPC

### Filing IRS (Modelo 3) — Personal Income Tax

1. Login with your personal NIF
2. Navigate: IRS > Entregar Declaração > Modelo 3
3. The system pre-fills known data (salaries, bank interest, invoices you've confirmed on e-fatura)
4. You must add:
   - Rental income (Anexo F) for each property — gross rent, allowable expenses, property NIF
   - Foreign income from Israel/Italy if applicable (Anexo J)
   - Capital gains from property sales (Anexo G)
5. Review the simulation (simulação) before submitting — it shows your refund or amount owed
6. Submit. You'll receive a submission receipt (comprovativo de entrega)
7. Deadline: April 30 each year (for income from the previous calendar year)
8. Refunds typically paid by end of July if filed early

### Filing IRC — Company Tax (HomeAid)

1. Login with HomeAid's NIPC (or access via representative)
2. Navigate: IRC > Entregar Declaração > Modelo 22
3. Prepared by your accountant (contabilista certificado) — this is mandatory for companies
4. Deadline: July 31 of the year following the fiscal year
5. Pagamentos por conta (advance payments) are made in July, September, and December based on prior year IRC

### IVA (VAT) Quarterly Submissions

1. Login with HomeAid's NIPC
2. Navigate: IVA > Entregar Declaração Periódica
3. Quarterly schedule for SMEs: April (Q1), July (Q2), October (Q3), January (Q4)
4. Your accountant typically handles this — ensure they have portal access (via "Procuração Eletrónica")
5. Payment due same day as submission
6. Keep all purchase and sales invoices organised by quarter

### Getting a Tax Compliance Certificate (Certidão de Não Dívida)

1. Login > Serviços > Certidões > Solicitar Certidão
2. Select type: "Situação Tributária" (confirms no outstanding tax debt)
3. Choose scope: personal NIF or company NIPC
4. Generated immediately if your situation is clear; delays if there are pending payments
5. Valid for 6 months
6. Needed for: property transactions, bank loans, company contracts, permits

### Checking Your Tax Situation

1. Login > "O Meu Perfil" or "Consultar" section
2. Check: pending payments, open processes, prior year assessments
3. "Coleta de IRS" shows your assessed tax for each year
4. "Notas de Cobrança" shows amounts due and payment deadlines

### Setting Up a Payment Plan (Plano de Pagamento Prestacional)

1. Login > Pagamentos > Plano de Pagamentos (Prestações)
2. Available when you have an outstanding debt note (nota de cobrança)
3. Select the debt, choose number of instalments (up to 36 months depending on amount)
4. Requires no prior debt defaults — if you have enforcement proceedings (execução fiscal), contact AT directly
5. A garantia (guarantee/surety) may be required for larger amounts

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Missing the April 30 IRS deadline** — penalty starts at €200 and increases with delay
- **Not declaring all rental income** — AT cross-references Modelo 2 (landlord annual statement) with your Modelo 3; discrepancies trigger audits
- **Failing to confirm invoices on e-Fatura** — if vendors issue invoices against your NIF, confirm them in the e-Fatura portal by February 15 each year to claim deductions
- **Switching between personal NIF and company NIPC without noticing** — double-check the active entity before submitting anything
- **Not granting your accountant a Procuração Eletrónica** — without this they cannot file on your behalf; do this once via Serviços > Procurações
- **Missing IVA quarterly payments** — interest and penalties accrue from the due date; set calendar reminders
- **Assuming foreign income is exempt** — Israeli and Italian-source income may still need to be declared in Portugal (subject to double tax treaty provisions); confirm with your accountant
