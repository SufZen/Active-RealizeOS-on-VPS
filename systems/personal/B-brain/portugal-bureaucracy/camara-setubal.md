# Câmara Municipal de Setúbal

## What This Is

The Câmara Municipal de Setúbal is the local government authority responsible for urban planning, building permits, property licensing, and municipal taxation within the municipality of Setúbal. For anyone owning or renovating property in Setúbal, the Câmara is the central authority for all physical changes to buildings and for ensuring that properties have valid habitability status.

The Câmara also administers IMI (in coordination with AT) and receives Modelo 1 IMI submissions for property reassessments.

## When You Need This

- Planning any building works — renovation, extension, change of use
- Checking if works are permissible before committing to a project (PIP)
- Obtaining or confirming a habitation licence (licença de utilização / certificado de habitabilidade) for a property you own or are selling
- Appealing an IMI assessment via Modelo 1 IMI
- Requesting information about a property's legal use classification (urban plan — PDM)
- Obtaining a DAP (Declaração de Aptidão Profissional) or DCP (Declaração de Conformidade com o PDM)

## Required Documents

For most permit applications you will need:
- Caderneta predial (from AT) for the property
- Certidão de teor (from Conservatória) showing you as owner
- Architectural drawings (memória descritiva e peças desenhadas) — prepared by a licensed architect
- Building licence number if amending prior licensed works
- Coordenadas GPS / localização do prédio
- NIF (personal or HomeAid's NIPC depending on who owns the property)
- For PIP: written description of what you want to do (no drawings required)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Submitting a PIP (Pedido de Informação Prévia)

A PIP is the low-risk first step: you ask the Câmara "can I do X on this property?" before spending money on architects and full permit applications.

1. Access the online portal: setubal.pt > Urbanismo > Pedido de Informação Prévia
2. Alternatively, use the Balcão Único (one-stop counter) at the Câmara building in Setúbal
3. Fill the form describing your intention (description of works, location, proposed use)
4. Attach: caderneta predial, certidão de teor, location sketch
5. Pay the municipal fee (taxa) — typically €100-300 for a PIP
6. Wait for a written opinion (despacho): 30-45 days typical
7. A positive PIP is not a licence, but it locks in the planning rules for 1 year and significantly de-risks the full application

### Applying for a Building Renovation Permit (Licença de Obras / Comunicação Prévia)

Two tracks depending on the scale of works:

**Comunicação Prévia (simplified — for most standard renovations):**
1. Hire a licensed architect to prepare the project
2. Submit via the online portal or Balcão Único:
   - Architectural project (memória descritiva + peças desenhadas)
   - Thermal compliance (REH or RECS) declaration if changing thermal envelope
   - Fire safety declaration (if applicable)
   - Stability project for structural works
3. The Câmara has 20 days to raise objections; if none, you can start works
4. Post-works: submit a Comunicação de Conclusão de Obras

**Licença de Obras (full licence — for larger or sensitive works):**
1. Same submission package as above, but also includes specialties projects (electrical, plumbing, etc.)
2. Câmara has 30-60 days to decide
3. Upon approval, pay the licença fee (varies significantly by project value)
4. Begin works — keep the licence on site
5. Upon completion: request a Vistoria (final inspection) and then the Licença de Utilização / Certificado de Habitabilidade

### Obtaining a Habitation Certificate (Licença de Utilização / Certificado de Habitabilidade)

Required when selling a property, renting it formally, or after completing licensed works.

1. Submit request to Câmara > Urbanismo > Licença de Utilização
2. Required: proof of completed works (comunicação de conclusão), as-built drawings if changed, energy performance certificate (Certificado de Desempenho Energético — CDE, issued by ADENE at adene.pt)
3. Câmara schedules a vistoria (physical inspection) — typically within 2-4 weeks
4. Inspector checks works match the approved plans and building codes
5. If approved: Licença de Utilização (or Autorização de Utilização) issued within 10 business days
6. Keep the original — you will need it for every property sale and formal rental contract

### IMI Assessment and Appeals (Modelo 1 IMI)

If you believe your property's VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário) is incorrect or has changed due to renovations:

1. Submit Modelo 1 IMI to the local Serviço de Finanças (AT), not the Câmara directly
2. However, the Câmara is where you get supporting documentation: building permit, vistoria approval
3. AT will reassess the property based on the model
4. If you want to formally challenge an existing assessment: submit a Reclamação within 30 days of the IMI notice
5. If the reclamação fails: appeal (impugnação judicial) within 90 days

### Accessing Municipal Services Online

1. setubal.pt — main Câmara website
2. Look for "Balcão Virtual" or "Portal do Munícipe" for online submissions
3. Many requests now accept electronic submission — upload documents and track status
4. Some specialised requests (historic zone works, significant demolition) still require in-person Balcão Único visits

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Starting works without any submission** — even "small" renovations to exterior finishes or structural elements require at minimum a Comunicação Prévia. Fines can exceed €5,000 and you may be ordered to undo the works
- **Not checking the PDM zoning** before buying a property for a specific use — the urban plan may prohibit what you have in mind (e.g., commercial use in a residential zone)
- **Letting the licença de utilização lapse or go missing** — without it, a property sale can be blocked at the notary (older properties built before 1951 may have an exemption, but confirm)
- **Skipping the energy certificate (CDE)** — mandatory for every property sale and formal rental; issued by ADENE, not the Câmara, but must be ready before the sale deed
- **Hiring unlicensed workers for licensed works** — any person signing off on a project must be a registered professional (OA for architects, OE for engineers)
- **Not informing the Câmara of change of use** — converting residential to commercial (or adding a short-term rental unit, AL — Alojamento Local) requires a separate permit or registration
