How many abandoned properties are there in Portugal?

Keep reading - you might be surprised

To cut it short: The issue of abandoned properties + impending legislation = investment opportunities.

It's enough to take a short walk in one of the main cities in Portugal to understand the extent of the problem. Abandoned apartments and houses alongside well-maintained and vibrant properties.

But no matter how many walks you take, it seems you won't be able to understand why. Why does a country with tremendous demand for real estate have an overwhelming number of abandoned properties?

We're not the only ones asking this question. The Portuguese government itself is asking the same question. And it has quite a few reasons to do so.

What's the answer? Well, there are several.

Disagreements over inherited properties, legal or administrative barriers due to bureaucracy, lack of financial capability for property renovation, and perhaps the main and most surprising reason - the minimal cost of holding an abandoned property. How minimal? In certain cases, just a few euros per year.

So why make the effort to bring a property back to the market when the bureaucratic process is complex and the cost of holding an abandoned property is so minimal?

The upcoming government program - Mais Habitação (More Housing) - is precisely the reason.

Mais Habitação is a comprehensive program aimed at easing the housing crisis developing in Portugal and solving the issue of abandoned properties.

The program's clauses are numerous and have sparked controversy, but it's clear to everyone that the program will significantly encourage the return of abandoned properties to the real estate market.

How? These are just some of the proposed clauses of the program:

- Increased property tax (IMI) on defined abandoned properties

- Benefits in property tax payments for

  properties that have been  redeveloped and returned to the market

- Reduction in property purchase tax (IMT) for

  properties acquired for improvement and reintegration into the market

- State participation in the expenses of

   rehabilitating abandoned properties

It can be seen that the program addresses almost every factor contributing to the issue of abandoned buildings. And within this lie interesting investment opportunities. But we'll delve into that next time.
